# Factions

> Organizations, governments, peoples, cults. Anyone with a collective agenda.

*Eight factions shape the world of Halig at game start. Some are crumbling, some are sealed, some are hidden, and one is a goddess wearing a half-orc's body. The free peoples have grief, distance, and each other. The corruption has organization, momentum, and Noctheris.*

---

## 1. House Valerian (Fallen)

- **Type:** Royal house — extinguished.
- **Headquarters:** Crownhaven (capital is rubble).
- **Leader(s):** **None.** The bloodline is genuinely gone. No surviving Valerians, no hidden bastards, no fostered cousin in a far hall. The line that began with Alaric and Seraphina has ended.
- **Goals:** *(historical)* Unified human realm under the Creator's faith.
- **Methods:** Faith, law, courtship of regional powers, the Founders' Vision as a unifying idea.
- **Resources:** Once: standing army, treasury, the Crownhaven hearth-fire, 500 years of accumulated trust. Now: ash and memory.
- **Allies:** Once: the Greenfields, the Demesne villages, the human peoples broadly. Cordial relations with Elenathir.
- **Enemies:** Ro'ak (catastrophically).
- **Public reputation:** Beloved. The Founding Festival was a national holiday. The Valerians are remembered as the kindest dynasty in mortal memory — flawed, occasionally weak, but *theirs.*
- **Internal conflicts:** N/A — they're gone.
- **Player relationship:** Ghosts the party walks among. Banners in the rubble. Songs at hearths. Their absence is a constant ache.

> **The Valerian Loyalists** *(diaspora, not a faction)*
> Some survivors of Crownhaven escaped — knights without lords, priests without altars, household staff without a hall. **They are not trying to rebuild the crown.** There is no crown to rebuild. They drift west and south, take refuge in villages, swear quiet oaths to memory rather than throne. The party will meet some of them. None of them know what to do.

## 2. Ro'ak's Host

- **Type:** Corrupted army.
- **Headquarters:** Ro'ak's fortress in Dreadvik (above the unsealed crack).
- **Leader(s):** **Ro'ak**, the half-orc warlord, vessel of Noctheris. Beneath him: **a handful of named lieutenants** *(TBD — to be developed in `04_characters.md`)* who command divisions of the host — turned humans, fallen elves, beast-handlers, necromancers, drake-riders. Each is a boss fight. Each is a piece of the journey east.
- **Goals:**
  - **Public/visible:** Conquer Eadenreach.
  - **Ro'ak's stated belief:** Burn the Creator's kingdoms, free the Old Gods, remake the world.
  - **Noctheris's actual goal *(true villain, hidden):*** Take every mortal child she can. Spread the unmaking. Devour what cannot bear.
- **Methods:** Slaughter and corruption. Cities burn; some inhabitants rise again to march east in the host's tail. The army grows as it advances.
- **Resources:** Wyverns, undead, elementals, beasts, witches, golems, vampires, liches, ogres, turned mortals. The full bestiary of Noctheris's making. *(Detailed in `02_history.md`, "The Present Day.")*
- **Allies:** Some orc clans of the Eastern Wilds; isolated cults (the Mothers, in scattered places). No formal alliances — the host *takes,* it does not negotiate.
- **Enemies:** Everyone living, eventually.
- **Public reputation:** Terror. *"The east is moving."* Refugees describe the host in fragments — different fragments by different witnesses, because no one sees the whole of it and lives.
- **Internal conflicts:** The lieutenants do not love each other. Some are loyal to Ro'ak. Some are loyal to Noctheris through Ro'ak. Some are loyal only to the appetites Noctheris has unlocked in them. **The party may be able to exploit this.**
- **Player relationship:** The primary antagonist force. Every region's mid-boss is a Ro'ak lieutenant. The march east is a march through his command structure.

## 3. The Council of Elenathir

- **Type:** Elven ruling body — multi-seat council, not a monarchy.
- **Headquarters:** Elenathir, the elven capital deep in the Old Forest. **Sealed. Gates shut. Players will not enter.**
- **Leader(s):** **The Council of Elenathir** — multiple voices. Names and seats TBD. The council speaks with one decision but rarely with one heart.
- **Goals:** Hold the line around Elenathir. Keep corruption out of the heart of the Old Forest. Preserve elven knowledge, elven tongue, elven faith for whatever world comes after this one.
- **Methods:** Defense, scouting, ranger patrols on the forest's outer edge. Magic — elves have older traditions of belief in the Creator (whom they call **the Star**) and their mages are formidable. But they are stretched.
- **Resources:** Disciplined warriors, ranger corps, mages, the city's natural defenses. Long memory. Patience.
- **Allies:** Historically warm to House Valerian. Wary of orcs. Largely indifferent to dwarves (who left long ago anyway). Right now: **no allies in the field** — they have no strength to spare for anyone else's war.
- **Enemies:** Ro'ak's host, the corruption itself.
- **Public reputation:** Among humans: distant, beautiful, slow to act, slow to forgive. Trustworthy when committed. Among themselves: *we are losing.*
- **Internal conflicts:** Council members disagree on whether to **hold tight at Elenathir** or **send a force out** to help the human realms. The "hold" faction is winning. The "send" faction is shrinking but not silent. Some council members may quietly support the party despite the official position.
- **Player relationship:** **Allies of necessity, not strength.** The party will meet **elven scouts and rangers on the forest's outer edge** — exhausted, willing to share what they know, willing to fight beside the party against corrupted enemies, but unable to spare more than a handful at a time. A council messenger may make contact in a key moment. The gates of Elenathir stay shut.

## 4. The Greenfields

- **Type:** A people, not a polity. Loosely-organized farming villages bound by shared culture, shared faith, and now shared loss.
- **Headquarters:** None. The Greenfields is a region, not a capital. **The closest thing to a center is now the Council of Elders** — see below.
- **Leader(s):** **The Council of Greenfields Elders** — an informal council of village elders and mayors that meets across villages when needed. Has met perhaps a dozen times in living memory. **Has now begun meeting weekly** in the wake of Fernfield's burning. They have no army. They have no decree. They have neighbors, hands, and decisions.
- **Goals:** Survive. Rebuild. Take in refugees. Hold the Greenfields together as a place worth being from.
- **Methods:** Shared labor, shared bread, shared grief. Rebuilding Fernfield is the council's first project — a gesture of defiance and a mercy to its survivors. **The party's village rises again because the council decided it would.**
- **Resources:** Hands, timber, grain, hearts. No standing army. A handful of old veterans who fought in border skirmishes decades ago. Local militias forming slowly.
- **Allies:** Scattered Valerian loyalists who pass through. Each other.
- **Enemies:** Ro'ak's host (when it comes for them). For now, they are west enough that the host has not yet reached the deep Greenfields.
- **Public reputation:** *"Greenfields folk look after their own."* True, and beautiful, and not enough against what's coming.
- **Internal conflicts:** Some elders argue they should flee farther west. Some argue they should fortify. The council is divided — but it is **acting,** which is more than most of Eadenreach can say.
- **Player relationship:** **Home.** The faction the party fights for. Letters from home. Caravans of supplies arriving in Fernfield's clearing while the party is far to the east. The village rebuilds because of them.

## 5. The Mothers

- **Type:** Cult of Noctheris. **Not organized.** No hierarchy, no temples, no robes, no creed. Connected only by their connection to her.
- **Headquarters:** None. **The Mothers are wherever pregnant women are alone.**
- **Leader(s):** **None.** Noctheris herself is the only thread between them, and she does not speak to them in council — she speaks to each one alone, in the dark, in dreams or in moments of weakness.
- **Goals:** Each Mother wants what she came for: **power.** A son who will love her. A husband who will not stray. A village that will respect her. Wealth. Beauty. A cure for grief. Whatever the woman wanted that the world denied her.
- **Methods:** **The bargain.** A pregnant woman, alone with her despair, hears a voice. The voice promises. The price is the unborn child. Some say yes. **The child is taken before its first breath** — miscarriage in the night, no body, no grave. The mother wakes with what she asked for, or thinks she does.
- **Resources:** **The power Noctheris grants is small.** Far smaller than she promises. A man's love that lasts a season. A village's respect that fades when the next harvest fails. A son who is not the son they meant. The Mothers do not realize this until much later — and by then there is nothing to give back. **Noctheris has the child. The Mother has a trinket.**
- **Membership:** **Always women. Always expecting mothers** at the moment of the bargain. Noctheris speaks to no one else.
- **Knowledge of Noctheris:** **Yes — they know her name.** She tells them. She wants them to know who they are kneeling to. The bargain is not anonymous; it is intimate.
- **Allies:** None — they don't know other Mothers exist, mostly. Each thinks her bargain is hers alone.
- **Enemies:** No active enemies. They are not a threat to the kingdom in any organized way. But each Mother is a **wound in the Creator's faith** somewhere — a quiet one, sometimes the wife of a priest, sometimes the matron of a respected family.
- **Public reputation:** **Whispered about.** Most people in Eadenreach know the name *the Mothers* the way they know any old ghost story. *"Don't go to the Wanting Mother when your prayers go unanswered."* Most who whisper it think it's superstition. It isn't.
- **Internal conflicts:** Each Mother eventually realizes the bargain was bad. Some die quietly with the secret. Some try to find a way to undo it. Some go mad. **A Mother who has realized what she did is one of the most haunted figures in Halig** — and one of the most valuable potential allies, if the party can reach her before she breaks.
- **Player relationship:** **A possible side-thread.** The party may meet a Mother in the Greenfields, the Demesne, or even at the edge of Dreadvik. She may help them — out of remorse, out of revenge against the goddess who cheated her. She may be a tragedy the party cannot save. Either way, she is one of the few mortal voices who can confirm what Noctheris truly is.

## 6. The Priests of the Red Chalice *(foreshadowed only — sequel)*

- **Type:** Cult of Morhaem.
- **Status in this game:** **Whispered about. Not encountered.** Coastal sailors and old scholars mention them in the same breath as the Mothers — *"there are worse cults than the Mothers, you know"* — and then change the subject. A traveler from a far southern port may carry a darker rumor. A priest at a Founding Festival may warn against them in a sermon nobody pays attention to. **The party does not meet them in this game.**
- **Why they exist in this file:** So that when they take the stage in the sequel, they will already have been a name the player has heard.

## 7. The Orc Clans of the Eastern Wilds

- **Type:** Tribal peoples — **many small clans, no central leader, fragmented.**
- **Headquarters:** Scattered camps and seasonal holds across the eastern wilds, between the Demesne moors and Dreadvik's foothills. No fixed cities.
- **Leader(s):** **No central leader.** No high chief, no warlord-of-warlords. Each clan has its own headman, its own honor code, its own grievances.
- **Goals:** Different by clan. Most: survive, hold their territory, honor their dead. Some: opportunity in the chaos. A few: outright joined Ro'ak.
- **Methods:** Raiding, hunting, herding, war by old codes. Most orc clans fight with discipline and ritual — they are not the savage hordes of human storybooks. **Honorable orcs exist, in numbers.**
- **Resources:** Strength, hardiness, tracking, knowledge of the eastern lands. No cities to defend, which is also no cities to lose.
- **Allies:** Each other, sometimes. Mostly themselves.
- **Enemies:** **Each other, often.** Centuries of clan grudges. Some clans hate the human realms; some have traded with them for generations.
- **Public reputation:** Among westerners: *raiders, savages, dangerous.* Mostly wrong, mostly fearful. Among themselves: *the world's last honest people.*
- **Internal conflicts:** **The Ro'ak question.** Some clans have joined his host outright — drawn by the promise of power, or corrupted, or simply cornered. Most have not. Some are **actively opposing** the host — small clans who refuse to march under a half-orc warlord whose father's name they don't know and whose mother was a human. The party may find unexpected allies among them.
- **Player relationship:** **Mixed encounters.** The party will fight some orcs. The party may find themselves shoulder-to-shoulder with others against a common Ro'ak warband. The orc clans are a reminder that **the lines of this war are not racial** — they're a matter of who has stayed faithful to their own honor, and who has not.

## 8. The Dwarves of the Far North

- **Type:** Lost people — silent for 1,000 years.
- **Headquarters:** Unknown. Somewhere in the far north. No envoy, no letter, no trader has come south since the Hollowing.
- **Leader(s):** Unknown. Whether they still have a king, a council, or anyone at all is **a question this game does not answer.**
- **Goals:** Unknown.
- **Methods:** Unknown.
- **Resources:** Unknown — but their forge-work was unmatched, and what they took with them could equip a dozen kingdoms.
- **Allies:** None known.
- **Enemies:** None known.
- **Public reputation:** *"The dwarves left."* That's the whole reputation. Some humans grew up with grandparents who knew dwarves; most did not. Children today think of them as a story.
- **Internal conflicts:** Unknown.
- **Player relationship:** **None in this game.** They are a cold mystery in the north. The party will not meet them. **They are a thread for a future story.** The crack they left behind in Dreadvik, however — that, the party will face directly.

**The corruption side:**

```
                       NOCTHERIS
                          ↑
                     feeds on
                          ↑
        +-----------------+------------------+
        |                 |                  |
   Ro'ak's Host    The Mothers       (foreshadowed)
        |          (private              Priests of
        |           bargains,             the Red
        |           unconnected)          Chalice
        |
   + some orc clans (joined)
```

**The free peoples side:**

```
   House Valerian (gone) → loyalist diaspora (drifting, no goal)
                ↘
                  cordial historical ties
                ↗
   Council of Elenathir — gates shut, holding the Old Forest
                ↕
                  no active alliance, but no hostility
                ↕
   Greenfields Council — rebuilding Fernfield, no military
                ↕
                  possible allies
                ↕
   Some orc clans — refusing Ro'ak, fighting their own war
                
   Dwarves of Far North — silent, unknown
```

**Reading the map:** the corruption has structure — a vessel, a host, a goddess, a quiet cult feeding her, and a darker cult waiting in the wings. The free peoples have **memory and proximity**, but no command, no banner, and no shared plan. The party walks the seam between them.
