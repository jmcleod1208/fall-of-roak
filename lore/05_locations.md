# Locations — Eadenreach Gazetteer

> The named places of *Fall of Ro'ak*, organized west to east along the journey from the burned village to the throne of the Wanting Mother's vessel. Eleven places. Each one earns its name.

The party's road runs from **the Greenfields** through **the Old Forest**, across **the Demesne**, and finally to **Dreadvik** on the eastern coast. The Crystal Waters river threads through the western half of the map, and the **Crown Road** — the old Valerian king's road, half-broken now — runs the length of the journey.

---

## Part 1 — The Greenfields

The home region. Rolling hills, mixed fields, mixed weather. England-flavor: hedgerows, stone walls, slate roofs, hearthsmoke. The land is good. The people are practical. The Creator is worshipped openly here; the Old Gods are rumors out of grandmother's tales.

### Fernfield
- **Type:** Burned village (formerly thriving market village; founded 400 AC; razed Spring 500 AC).
- **Region:** Western Greenfields.
- **Controlled by:** Nominally Hartwick's reeve; in practice self-governed by the village elders. After the burning: **nothing.** Ash and crows.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** ~600 before the burning. ~140 survivors who fled downriver to Hartwick. The rest dead or taken.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:**
  - *Before:* The smell of hearth-smoke and baking bread; the sound of children in the lanes, the mill-wheel turning, geese on the river. A place on its way to becoming a town. Maybe one day, a city.
  - *After:* The wet stink of cold ash and rotting thatch. Crows. The Crystal Waters still runs clean past the burned timbers, indifferent.
- **Visual signature:** **Blackened timber frames standing in the green fields**, the mill-wheel half-burnt and turning slowly in the river, the grain still ripening in the unburned fields beyond — *the harvest no one came back for.*
- **History:**
  - **Founded 400 AC** by Greenfields settlers who recognized the bend in the Crystal Waters river as good country for grain and grazing.
  - Grew over the next century from a hamlet to a true market village. Its annual **Wheat Fair** drew traders from as far as Hartwick and beyond.
  - **The Skye family** has lived here for three generations; Vander de la Cruz's family for two.
  - **Spring 500 AC: Ro'ak's host strikes.** Ra'gor's old clan — now Ro'ak's vanguard — sweeps across the Crystal Waters fords at first light. The village burns. The chapel burns. **George and Sophie de la Cruz** die inside it. Runlan, Calypsa, Vallen, and Van are out hunting / on the river / at the eastern hedgerow when it begins. They return to a column of smoke.
- **Geography:**
  - Sits on a **bend of the Crystal Waters river**, fields of wheat and barley spreading on either side.
  - The **western mill** stands at the river's edge; the **eastern hedgerow** marks the village's farthest cottage. A single stone bridge crosses the river to the southern grazing land.
  - The **Crown Road** runs through the village's market square — the road that runs all the way east to fallen Crownhaven and beyond, to Dreadvik.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The market square** — cobbled, ringed by stalls now ash. The well in the center is still good.
  - **The chapel of the Creator** — burned to its stone foundations. The bell fell and is half-melted. *George and Sophie's bodies were never recovered.*
  - **The Skye cottage** — at the eastern edge, the last cottage before the hedgerow. Partly standing; the kitchen survived. Calypsa's herb garden is trampled but alive.
  - **The mill** — half-burnt; the wheel still turns.
  - **The bridge** — intact. Stone. Older than the village; the Greenfields settlers built around it.
- **Notable NPCs here (or who were here):**
  - The four heroes (their home).
  - **George and Sophie de la Cruz** (dead).
  - **The Fernfield elder** (TBD — Part 4 of `04_characters.md`).
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **Opening of the game.** The burning is the inciting incident.
  - The player may return mid-game to bury the dead, recover heirlooms from the Skye cottage, or check on the rebuild (village-building mechanic).
- **Secrets:**
  - Beneath the chapel's stone foundation, a small **iron coffer** sealed by Van's father George — the de la Cruz family's holy relics. Untouched by the fire. Van does not know it exists. *(Sequel hook? Mid-game discovery?)*
  - The grain in the unburned eastern fields is still ripening. **If the rebuild is fast enough, Fernfield will have a harvest this year.** If not, the crows will.

### Hartwick
- **Type:** Market town (population ~3,200 before the war).
- **Region:** Central Greenfields, downriver from Fernfield along the Crystal Waters.
- **Controlled by:** **Reeve Aldwin Marsh** (a Hartwick civic post, more steward than lord) and a small Crownhaven-sworn garrison. With Crownhaven fallen, the garrison takes orders from no one but its captain.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** ~3,200 before; swelling now with refugees from Fernfield and the surrounding burned hamlets. Greenfields commoners — millers, smiths, merchants, river-traders, a small Creator-priesthood.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** The bustle of a market town overloaded by refugees. Tents and lean-tos clogging the back streets, the well-lines long, prices high, tempers short. **The chapel bells ring at every hour because someone is always grieving someone.** And yet — Hartwick has not been attacked. The smoke is on the western horizon, not in the streets. There is still bread.
- **Visual signature:** **A river town at dusk with the chapel bells ringing**, refugee tents lining the Crown Road, the Crystal Waters running clean and indifferent past the docks, the western sky red with distant fires.
- **History:**
  - Founded centuries before Fernfield as a Valerian-era trading post at the river's widest point.
  - Grew into the central market for the western Greenfields. Fernfield's grain, the coastal village's fish, and the merchants' wares from the Crown Road all met here.
  - **Van's parents George and Sophie were married in Hartwick's chapel** before moving upriver to Fernfield. Van was born here.
  - With Crownhaven's fall (a few weeks before the game begins), Hartwick has become the **de facto authority** of the western Greenfields — though it doesn't want the role.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Market Square** — twice the size of Fernfield's, with a stone fountain at its center.
  - **The Old Chapel** — large stone chapel, the bells ring at every hour, currently overcrowded with refugees sleeping in the nave.
  - **The Crown Bridge** — three stone arches across the Crystal Waters; the second-oldest structure in town.
  - **Reeve's Hall** — a half-timbered building on the square where Aldwin Marsh holds court.
  - **The garrison's tower** — squat, four stories, on the riverbank. Holds maybe 60 swords.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **Reeve Aldwin Marsh** — the man trying to hold the town together.
  - The Hartwick garrison captain (TBD).
  - Refugees including Fernfield survivors.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **After Fernfield burns**, this is where the survivors flee — and where the heroes anchor the rebuild effort before heading east.
  - **Recruiting & resupply** for the road east.
  - **The village-building mechanic** routes through Hartwick: trade, hires, materials.
  - The chapel where Van's parents were married — a quiet moment of grief for him.
- **Secrets:**
  - The Hartwick garrison captain is a former Valerian soldier who fled Crownhaven's fall. He has not told the reeve everything he saw.
  - **A small Mother shrine** is hidden in the storerooms beneath one of the merchant warehouses. Three Hartwick women attend it.

### The Crystal Waters & the Western Coast
- **Type:** River and coastal region.
- **Region:** Western Greenfields.
- **Controlled by:** No one in particular. Free folk.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Fishing villages, river-traders, a few hermits along the upper reaches.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** Cool river-mist mornings; gulls along the coast; the smell of fish-smoke and tar. The Crystal Waters runs **clear** (its name is earned) from somewhere in the Old Forest's western foothills down to the coast.
- **Visual signature:** **The river meeting the sea at dawn**, a small village of slate-roofed cottages, fishing boats drawn up on a pebbled beach, gulls turning overhead.
- **History:**
  - **Calypsa Skye was born in a fishing village on this coast** — her father **Francis** the fisherman, her mother **Alexandra** the fishmonger. The village has no formal name; locals call it *the Bay.*
  - Calypsa heard her first **Aqueloth tales** here as a child — the sailors' rumors of the Hullbreaker beneath the western sea. She thought them fiction. (She was wrong. *Sequel.*)
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Bay** — Calypsa's birthplace, a small fishing village at the river's mouth.
  - **The Crystal Source** — somewhere in the western Old Forest foothills, the river is fed by a spring the elves consider sacred. The party does not visit. (*Sequel hook.*)
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - Calypsa's parents (long dead by Year 500 AC).
  - A handful of unnamed fisherfolk who knew the Skye family.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **Optional side-area.** Calypsa can return to her birthplace for a personal scene; the player can recruit a fisher-guide.
  - Foreshadowing of the sea, the Hullbreaker, and the sequel.

## Part 2 — The Old Forest

East of the Greenfields. Ancient woodland untouched by the axe for a thousand years. The elves live here. Outsiders are not welcome past the outer reaches. The forest's interior is **closed** to humans — the elves enforce it.

### Greenwatch (Forest's Edge Outpost)
- **Type:** Active elven outpost on the western fringe of the Old Forest.
- **Region:** Western edge of the Old Forest, where it meets the eastern Greenfields.
- **Controlled by:** **The Old Forest elves** — specifically a small ranger-warden detachment under Council orders.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** ~40 elven rangers and scouts, rotated from Elenathir on long postings. A handful of human envoys and traders who have earned the right to camp here.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** Quiet, calm, watchful. The outpost is built **into** the living trees rather than on top of them — woven walkways, bark-clad lookouts, a single open hearth-circle at ground level where elves and visitors meet as equals. The air smells of pine resin and clean smoke. **Friendly, openly so.** The elves here are not hostile to humans — they're **forthcoming.** But they will not let you pass into the deep forest. That is a different conversation.
- **Visual signature:** **A great old oak with a woven elven hall built around its trunk**, lantern-warmth spilling from the upper galleries, a stone hearth-circle below where elves and humans sit together passing a pipe.
- **History:**
  - Greenwatch has stood since before Valerian fell — older than the kingdoms of men in this region.
  - The elves have always met outsiders here. **No human has set foot in Elenathir in five hundred years**, but every human delegation has been received at Greenwatch with full courtesy.
  - With Crownhaven fallen and Ro'ak's host moving, **the elven Council has chosen to receive the party openly and offer help.** This is unusual but not unprecedented. They have done it before, when the Five threatened the world.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Hearth-Circle** — open stone ring where the party is received and counseled by an elven ranger-captain.
  - **The Lookout Galleries** — carved into the upper trunk of the great oak; the elves watch the Crown Road from up there.
  - **The Quartermaster's Hollow** — where the party can be re-equipped with elven gear: a finer bow for Runlan, herbs Calypsa cannot find in the Greenfields, light arrow-bundles.
  - **The Closed Path** — a footpath leading deeper into the forest, **guarded by two unsmiling sentinels.** It is not a question of malice. It is a question of law. *No.*
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **Captain Aerinith** — the elven ranger-captain who receives the party at the Hearth-Circle. Calm, careful, courteous in the old way. He is the senior officer at Greenwatch, the maker of the grove-tokens the party carries deeper into the forest, and the man who reads the Crown Road from the upper galleries each night without speaking. He has been at Greenwatch longer than any human kingdom now standing.
  - A young elven scout who admires Vallen's twin-blade style (TBD — supporting NPC).
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **First major waypoint after Hartwick.** The elves give the party warning of what waits ahead — and confirm that Ser Kael Sunstrike is hunting them on the forest's eastern road.
  - **Resupply, intel, equipment.**
  - **Ser Kael's ambush happens on the road past Greenwatch**, after the party leaves the outpost.
- **Secrets:**
  - The elves know more about Noctheris than they have told. They know the dwarves cracked her prison. They know **one dwarf stayed.** They have not said his name aloud in a thousand years.
  - **Seraphiel Moonwhisper was once one of them** — a Council member. **Captain Aerinith knew her**, served under her in the old Council guard, loved her once in the quiet elven way that does not require speaking it. He does not speak of her now. *Don't ask.* If the party returns to Greenwatch after the Cradle carrying her circlet and the letter she left him, Aerinith will receive them, read the letter alone, and that night sing the funeral cadence for an Elenathir Council member that has not been sung in three hundred years. Greenwatch is quieter for a week.

### Seraphiel's Cradle (Corrupted Glade)
- **Type:** Corrupted forest enclave. Active rot-source.
- **Region:** Deep Old Forest, between Greenwatch and Elenathir.
- **Controlled by:** **Seraphiel Moonwhisper** (in absentia — she is more often at Ro'ak's side, but the Cradle is hers in soil and bone). Her fallen-elf cohort and corrupted forest creatures hold it in her name.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Fallen elves of her household (wardens, bladesingers, thornsingers), Cradle Acolytes (mortal cultists), corrupted wildlife, the Briarchild, and — in a glade a short distance away — **Aetherion the Green**, sleeping no longer.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **Wrong-quiet.** The songbirds are gone. The moss glows faintly violet at night. The trees lean slightly inward. The air tastes of wet iron and something sweet that should not be sweet. **The corruption here is centered, not spreading by accident** — you can feel the source the way you feel a low fever in another room.
- **Visual signature:** **A once-sacred elven glade with a single great tree at its heart**, the tree's bark split with violet eldritch fissures, the surrounding wood leaning toward it as if listening. Roots have grown in shapes a tree does not grow in.
- **History:**
  - The glade was a sacred elven site — a Council ritual ground from before Crownhaven was founded. **Seraphiel knew the wards.** That is how she carved it.
  - After fleeing Elenathir, she came here first. She seeded the corruption slowly so the Council would not detect a single act of betrayal until the wood itself began to rot. **Years of patience.**
  - **Aetherion's compromise began here.** His sleeping glade is close enough that her gift reached him through soil and water. She never attacked him. She poisoned the ground he lay on.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Heart Tree** — the split great tree at the center. Seraphiel's anchor in the wood. Damaging it weakens the entire region's corruption.
  - **The Acolyte Camps** — ring of small camps around the Heart Tree where mortal Cradle Acolytes tend the rot.
  - **The Approach Paths** — winding game-trails marked by Thorned Greatwood guardians; Greenwatch's grove-tokens map them.
  - **Aetherion's Glade** — a short distance away, where the wyrm slept and now rages. The party may fight the wyrm before reaching the Cradle itself, depending on path.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - Seraphiel's lieutenants (TBD — supporting NPCs).
  - **Aetherion**, in his adjacent glade.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **Region 2 storyline anchor.** Greenwatch will mark paths; the party closes on the Cradle through Seraphiel-aligned territory.
  - **Aetherion's fight** is staged in the adjacent glade.
  - **Seraphiel herself** is not fought here — she is at Ro'ak's side. But damaging the Heart Tree denies her this corner of the wood for the rest of the campaign.
- **Secrets:**
  - **The Heart Tree was Seraphiel's own coronation-grove** in centuries past, when she was still a Council member. She corrupted the place that crowned her. The elves of Greenwatch know this and will not speak of it.
  - Beneath the roots, **a small offering-pit** holds the bones of every creature Seraphiel has personally killed in this wood. The pit is older than the corruption. *(What did she kill before she fell? Sequel hook.)*

### Elenathir (Distant, Closed)
- **Type:** Elven capital city, hidden in the heart of the Old Forest.
- **Region:** Central Old Forest.
- **Controlled by:** **The Elven Council.**
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Unknown to outsiders. Tens of thousands, possibly.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** Glimpsed only in elven tales and corrupted memory — Seraphiel's ruined visions show towers of living woven trees, glow-lanterns, silver-leaf canopies. The party never sees it firsthand.
- **Visual signature:** **A city of living trees seen only in dream or vision** — silver canopies, woven walkways, lantern-light reflecting from a hundred small bridges.
- **History:**
  - Stood since before recorded history. Older than Valerian. Older than the Hollowing.
  - Has never fallen, never been taken, never been entered by an outsider in five centuries.
  - **Seraphiel Moonwhisper sat on its Council.** She is the reason the elves are quiet about her.
- **Points of interest:**
  - *Inaccessible.* Glimpsed in vision and second-hand tale only.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **The player does not.** Elenathir is the world's "closed door" — the city the player will *not* enter in this game.
  - *(Sequel hook: the door may open.)*

## Part 3 — The Demesne

The fallen kingdom of Valerian. Five hundred years ago a true kingdom — five centuries of Crownhaven's faith held the moor-mist at bay. **Then Crownhaven fell.** A few weeks ago. The mist has returned. The land is ruined moors, broken stone, mass graves, and corrupted nature. Vorthak the Crimson Destroyer roams above it.

### Crownhaven (Fallen, Still Burning)
- **Type:** Recently-fallen capital city, currently burning.
- **Region:** Central Demesne.
- **Controlled by:** **No one.** Ro'ak's host moved through it and left it broken. A few scattered survivors hide in the cellars. Carrion-things and cultist scavengers prowl the streets.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Once ~40,000. **Now a few hundred at most** — survivors hiding, the dying, scavengers, a handful of Mothers who have crept in to harvest the unclaimed.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **A city in mid-collapse.** The fires were set a few days before the party arrives, and many of them are *still burning* — fueled by spilled oil-stores, broken granaries, the city's own timber bones. Smoke columns visible from miles away. The air is hot, ash-thick, and tastes of soot and blood. Crows everywhere. **The bells in the high cathedral are still ringing — no one is ringing them. They ring because the cathedral is shaking as it slowly comes down.**
- **Visual signature:** **The Cathedral of the Sun still burning at the city's heart**, its golden spire blackened, its bells ringing without bellringers, smoke pouring from its arched windows, the city's broken walls silhouetted around it against an orange sky.
- **History:**
  - **Founded in Year 0 AC** as the capital of the Valerian kingdom — its founding is the anchor of the present calendar.
  - For five centuries, **its faith held back the Demesne mist.** The mist had ruled these moors before Valerian came; the kingdom's prayer-discipline pushed it out and kept it out.
  - **Crownhaven fell a few weeks before the game begins.** Ro'ak's host struck from the north and east at the same time. The defenders never had a chance to coordinate. The royal family was killed in the great hall. The high priest was killed at the altar. The city burned.
  - **The mist returned within hours of the fall.** It has not lifted since.
- **Geography:**
  - Sits on a high broken hill at the center of the Demesne moors, the Crown Road approaching from the west and continuing to the east toward Dreadvik.
  - Three concentric rings of broken walls. Most of the outer ring is breached.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Cathedral of the Sun** — half-collapsed, still burning. The bells still ring. The great stained-glass windows of the Creator and the Sun are mostly shattered; the few remaining panels still glow in the firelight, defiant. **Van will pray here.** The party may find the dead high priest at the altar, his hand empty but his face calm.
  - **The Royal Hall** — sacked. Bodies still in their seats. The throne is split down the middle by a single axe-stroke.
  - **The Black Library** — Crownhaven's archive of forbidden texts. Partly burnt, partly intact. Whatever the high priest hid here, the fires have begun to uncover.
  - **The Crown Granaries** — these are what is still burning. Underground oil and grain stores; the fires will burn for weeks.
  - **The Sunken Quarter** — the lowest district, flooded by the broken aqueduct. Survivors hide here.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **A surviving Valerian loyalist** (knight or priest) the party meets in the wreckage (TBD — supporting NPC).
  - **A Crownhaven refugee or two** still alive in the cellars (TBD — supporting NPC).
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **Major mid-game story location.** The party must cross Crownhaven to continue east on the Crown Road.
  - **Black Library research:** uncovering what the Valerian priesthood knew about Noctheris and the Old Gods — hard-won knowledge about how a vessel like Ro'ak might be unmade.
  - **Honoring the dead:** the cathedral and the Royal Hall are sites of grief for Van especially (a fellow Creator-faithful). The party may light the candles, perform last rites, recover relics.
- **Secrets:**
  - **Crownhaven was faithful to the Creator to the end.** The royal family died with prayers on their lips. The high priest fell at the altar. This was not a city rotted from within — it was a city overwhelmed from without. **That is what makes it so terrible.** Faith was not enough.
  - The high priest's body **is** at the altar. But what he was holding when he died is gone. Someone took it from his hand after he fell. *(Who? Sequel?)*

### The Demesne Moors
- **Type:** Ruined wilderness, mist-cloaked moorlands.
- **Region:** Surrounding Crownhaven on all sides; stretches east to the foothills of Dreadvik.
- **Controlled by:** **Vorthak the Crimson Destroyer**, in practice. The dragon hunts here. He answers to no one but Ro'ak.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Scattered. Hermits, refugee bands, a few half-mad Mother shrines, the occasional Valerian deserter. Wildlife corrupted by the returned mist — twisted wolves, mist-elk, things that should not be wolves at all.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **Cold, grey, foggy, low.** Heather and gorse stretching to a horizon that is never clear. A constant low wind. The sun is a pale disk. Sound carries oddly through the mist — a horse hoof half a mile away sounds like it is at your shoulder. **You hear Vorthak's wings before you see him. By then it is too late.**
- **Visual signature:** **The dragon's silhouette in the mist overhead**, briefly, his wings folding past — and then gone again. The mist closing behind him.
- **History:**
  - These were the moors that the Valerian kings tamed and held with faith for five centuries.
  - **The mist returned within hours of Crownhaven's fall** and has been thickening since.
  - The corrupted wildlife is **days old**, not centuries. Something is changing it actively. *Noctheris's reach.*
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Crown Road** — half-broken, mile-stones tipped over, but still the only safe(r) path through the mist.
  - **The Standing Stones of Aelric** — pre-Valerian megaliths, a ring of seven, where a Mother shrine has recently been set up.
  - **The Mass Graves** — fresh, from Crownhaven's fall. The mist is thickest here.
  - **A roadside wayhouse** — half-burnt, partly habitable. A small refugee camp clusters here.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **A Mother** the party meets at the Standing Stones (TBD — supporting NPC).
  - **A Demesne hermit** if you include one (TBD — supporting NPC).
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **Cross the moors to reach Dreadvik.** Unavoidable.
  - **Vorthak's boss fight** happens in the moors, not at Crownhaven — he ambushes the party on the Crown Road east of the city.
- **Secrets:**
  - The Standing Stones of Aelric are **older than the Hollowing.** They predate the dwarves cracking Noctheris's prison. They were carved by a people who have no name in any human record.
  - **One of the corrupted mist-wolves is the high priest's hunting hound.** It will know the party if they carry anything from Crownhaven's cathedral.

## Part 4 — Dreadvik

The Scandinavian-flavor far east. Coastal fjords, dark pine forests, longhalls, thralls, raiders. **Ro'ak's seat.** His personal lands. The host that burned the west was raised here.

### The Black Spires (Ro'ak's Cliff-Fortress)
- **Type:** Cliff-fortress overlooking an eastern fjord.
- **Region:** Northeast coast of Dreadvik.
- **Controlled by:** **Ro'ak**, vessel of Noctheris.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Ro'ak's standing host — orc warriors, twisted human soldiers, Mothers in service, the half-broken thralls who clean and cook. Maybe two thousand armed within the fortress at any time. More in the surrounding camps.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **Cold, salt-spray, iron, smoke.** A black-stone fortress carved directly into a cliff-face, with tall pointed spires rising from the cliff-top like the fingers of a buried giant. The air is sharp, the sea below crashes on rocks, the wind never quite stops. **You hear the host before you see the gate — drums in the deep halls, the clang of iron, the chanting of the Mothers.**
- **Visual signature:** **The Black Spires silhouetted against an iron-grey eastern sea** — sharp pointed towers rising from a cliff that drops a hundred fathoms into the fjord, Ro'ak's banner of the violet eye flying from the highest spire, the Cinderforge gate at the cliff's base glowing dull-orange with Durgan's forges.
- **History:**
  - The cliff was a **dwarven hold** in the deep past — a remote eastern outpost of the same people who would later, far to the west under the mountains, crack Noctheris's prison in 1000 BC. The dwarves who lived here abandoned it long before the Hollowing, for reasons no record of any race remembers. **What they left behind, Noctheris found.** She has lurked in the cracked places beneath the cliff ever since — long before Ro'ak was born.
  - Centuries later the Dreadvik chieftains — Scandinavian-flavor fjord-lords — built their longhalls and feast-halls into the cliff's upper terraces, never knowing what slept below. They held it for generations.
  - **Ro'ak emerged from Noctheris's lair beneath the cliff in 499 AC** and took the surface holding for his own. He did not inherit it from his father — Ra'gor was a clan chieftain of the Eastern Wilds, far inland, and never saw this fjord. Ro'ak took the cliff because **his mother was already beneath it**, and the chieftains who held the surface could not stand against what she had spent 250 years making in him.
  - **For two centuries before he emerged, and for the year since, Ro'ak has been building.** The Cinderforge gate at the base. The inner halls deepening into the cliff and reconnecting to the old dwarven works. The spires climbing higher. **His High Hall at the summit, set directly above her prison-chamber.** **The Black Spires are his masterwork. Mother's Hearth, beneath them, is hers.**
- **Geography:**
  - The cliff faces northeast onto **the Mother's Fjord** (named after Noctheris by Ro'ak; the locals had a different name once).
  - The fjord opens directly onto **the Eastern Ocean** — the sea no one has crossed.
  - **The Cinderforge Gate** at the cliff's base is the only way in; it opens onto a long winding stair carved up through the cliff.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Cinderforge Gate** — Durgan's workshop and fighting ground; the first major obstacle.
  - **The Cliff Stair** — a thousand-step winding stair carved up the cliff face, half open to the sea winds. Walking it is a chapter of the game.
  - **The Lower Halls** — barracks, mess, training yards, the host's day-to-day life.
  - **Ro'ak's High Hall** (see below) — the throne hall at the cliff-top, where Ro'ak waits.
  - **The Spires** — six pointed towers rising from the cliff-top, used for signaling, scrying, and Mother rituals.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **Ro'ak**, in his High Hall.
  - **Durgan Cinderforge**, at the Gate.
  - Mothers, lieutenants, thralls.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **The endgame.** The party arrives in the dead of Winter 500 AC.
- **Secrets:**
  - The fortress is **Durgan's two-century masterwork.** Every stone of the deeper halls bears his rune-marks. He could bring it down himself if he chose to. He has never chosen to.
  - Beneath the lowest hall, **a sealed door no one alive has opened.** The Dreadvik chieftains sealed it before Ra'gor's clan came. *(What is behind it? Sequel hook.)*

### Ro'ak's High Hall (Throne Hall of the Black Spires)
- **Type:** Throne hall at the summit of the Black Spires.
- **Region:** Region 4 capstone. The peak of the cliff-fortress.
- **Controlled by:** **Ro'ak.** Through him, Noctheris.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Ro'ak. His personal guard (three of his strongest, always at the iron door). The Mothers who attend the hearth-pit. **And Noctheris, reaching up through the floor.**
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **Vast, open, and wrong.** A hall of black stone with a ceiling that rises into the spires above, lit by braziers of green-violet flame that throw long living shadows. The far wall is open to the eastern sea — no glass, no door, just a wide stone balcony where Ro'ak's throne sits **overlooking the fjord and the open ocean beyond.** The wind comes in cold and salty. The shadows on the walls do not always match the bodies casting them.
- **Visual signature:** **Ro'ak on his black-iron throne, axe across his knees, Noctheris's staff planted beside him, the open eastern sea stretching out behind him under a stormy sky.** The throne is built so he can look east at all times. He has been looking east for two centuries. He is waiting for her to come up from the deep.
- **History:**
  - Built by Ro'ak in the first century of his rule, over the old Dreadvik chieftains' feast-hall. The new floor is laid directly over the old, and the old floor is laid directly over the cracks that lead down to her.
  - **The Cinder-Hearth** — a great circular stone fire-pit in the center of the hall — burns with **green-violet flame** that never goes out. **It is not fire. It is presence.** It is an echo of the true Hearth far below. Noctheris reaches up through it; Ro'ak speaks to her down through it.
  - **This is where Ro'ak speaks to her aloud.** The Mothers attend. The flames respond.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **Ro'ak's Throne** — black iron, simple, brutal. Faces east. Set on a low dais of natural cliff-stone.
  - **The Cinder-Hearth** — circular fire-pit at the hall's center. Green-violet flame. The echo of Noctheris's true hearth below.
  - **The Eastern Balcony** — open stone shelf overhanging the fjord. No rail. A long fall.
  - **The Mother's Veil** — a curtain of dark gauze hanging behind the throne. Behind it is a **shrine to Noctheris** in her partial form — a statue with the constant violet eyes.
  - **The Iron Door** — west wall. The only entrance. Three of Ro'ak's strongest guards stand inside it at all times.
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **Ro'ak.** Always. **This is where he dies.**
  - **A rotating attendance of three Mothers** who tend the Cinder-Hearth.
  - **Two of his strongest guards** at the door.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **The Region 4 capstone.** The party fights through the Cinderforge Gate, climbs the Cliff Stair, crosses the Lower Halls, and reaches the Iron Door. Beyond it: Ro'ak.
  - **When Ro'ak dies, the Cinder-Hearth flares once and dies, and the floor cracks open.** Noctheris pulls his body down. The party can follow, or run.
- **Secrets:**
  - The **green-violet flame** of the Cinder-Hearth is fed by something. Mother offerings. Children. Bones beneath the floor. The party may see what during the fight.
  - **The Cinder-Hearth is Ro'ak's tether to Noctheris in this place.** Breaking the Cinder-Hearth during the fight weakens him — and tells her her vessel is dying. **She begins to wake.**
  - **When Ro'ak falls, the floor opens. The only way forward is down.**

## Part 5 — Beneath the Spires

### Mother's Hearth (Prison of Noctheris)
- **Type:** The prison chamber of Noctheris. **Final battle of the game.**
- **Region:** Region 5. Deep beneath the Black Spires, in the dark below the dwarven works, at the heart of the Hollowing.
- **Controlled by:** **Noctheris.** The prison is hers in the way a cell is the prisoner's.
- **Population / Inhabitants:** Noctheris. The cracked chain. The broken iron throne. Her prison-warders, twisted across three thousand years into something that no longer remembers what it was meant to guard. Sleeping dwarves preserved in the dark of the deepest halls. And, briefly, the body of Ro'ak — falling.
- **Vibe / Atmosphere:** **A vast chamber of black stone, walls split by violet eldritch fissures** that pulse like a heart. The ceiling is lost in the dark above. A throne of broken iron sits at the chamber's heart, a chain trailing from its arm, one shackle hanging open. The air is wrong — heavier than air should be, slow, tasting of iron and old smoke. Sound carries strangely. The party will hear their own footsteps a heartbeat after they take them.
- **Visual signature:** **The same chamber the party has glimpsed in dreams and visions throughout the whole game** — Ro'ak's dreams, the Mothers' rituals, cult shrines on the road, their own sleep as Noctheris reached for them. They have seen this place a hundred times before they ever stand in it. The walls are familiar. The throne is familiar. **And finally, looking up from the throne, Noctheris herself is real.**
- **History:**
  - **Carved before recorded history.** The Five were sealed here at the dawn of the world by the Creator. They have been here ever since.
  - **The dwarves cracked the prison in 1000 BC** — the **Hollowing** — and the crack has been widening for three thousand years. They fled north and vanished. Except Durgan. Durgan stayed.
  - **For 250 years before Ro'ak emerged, Noctheris shaped a child from the offering of a Mother given to her here, in this chamber, in this dark.** Ro'ak was made in Mother's Hearth.
  - Now the party is in it.
- **Points of interest:**
  - **The Broken Throne** — at the chamber's heart. The chain trailing from one arm. One shackle hanging open. The other still locked around the throne's leg.
  - **The Cracks** — violet fissures running floor to ceiling along the walls. They pulse. They are wider at the top than at the bottom.
  - **The Fall** — directly above the throne, the ceiling opens to a long dark shaft that climbs up through the Hollowing-broken stone, up through the lower halls of the Spires, up to the cracked floor of Ro'ak's High Hall. This is the way the party came in. This is the way they leave, if they leave.
  - **The Sleeping Halls** — the side galleries off the chamber, where dwarves who did not flee with their kin lie preserved in postures of work and worship. They do not wake. They are not exactly dead.
  - **The Watchers** — the cracks are wider at the top because **four other prisoners are pressed close to them, listening.** Visstheri. Aqueloth. Drakhalion. Morhaem. The party may glimpse a violet eye, a scaled cheek, a coil of hair behind the cracks, between phases of the fight. **The Watchers do not act. They watch. They are taking notes.**
- **Notable NPCs here:**
  - **Noctheris.** In her partial freedom. Half-out of the throne. Reaching.
  - **The Watchers**, behind the cracks. Silent. Inevitable.
- **Hooks / Why the player goes here:**
  - **The final battle of the game.** Noctheris herself, in several phases. **The party fights her in her own prison.**
- **Secrets:**
  - The shackle on her left wrist has been open since the Hollowing. The shackle on her right is still closed. **The chain on the throne still holds her — barely.** Breaking the chain during the fight is the last phase. The chain is what the Creator put on her at the dawn of the world. It is harder than any weapon, any spell, any faith.
  - **The Watchers are taking notes.** When the fight ends, they will know how to break their own bonds. *(Sequel hook. The four other Old Gods.)*
  - There is one detail in the chamber the party may not notice on the first pass: **a small figure seated cross-legged in the deepest shadow of the sleeping halls**, very still, very old, watching the fight. *Durgan's brother — the one other dwarf who stayed. He has been here for three thousand years. (Sequel hook. Or red herring. Author's choice when `09_plot.md` is written.)*
