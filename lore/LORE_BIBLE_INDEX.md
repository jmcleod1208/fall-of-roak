## Project at a glance

- **Title.** Fall of Ro'ak (working title).
- **Genre.** Story-driven fantasy auto-battler RPG.
- **Pitch.** Four heroes survive the burning of their village (Fernfield) and march east across the kingdom of Eadenreach to cut down Ro'ak, a half-orc warlord whose corrupting army threatens to swallow the world. The deeper truth: Ro'ak is the vessel of Noctheris, an old god in a cracked prison, using him to corrupt Halig from within. Killing Ro'ak is only the threshold of the real war.
- **Tone.** Grounded sword-and-sorcery, Witcher-adjacent. Medieval, lived-in, magic rare enough to feel wondrous and dangerous, dragons and old gods half-believed until they aren't.
- **Public GitHub repo.** https://github.com/jmcleod1208/fall-of-roak — contains the eleven lore files in `lore/`. No images committed; art direction is intentionally open.

## The antagonists

- **Ro'ak.** Half-orc warlord. The only Mother-given child ever to survive infancy and grow. Emerged from beneath Dreadvik in 499 AC. He is Noctheris's vessel.
- **Noctheris.** Old god in a cracked prison beneath the world. Speaks through Ro'ak. Reaches through her vessels. Wants out.
- **Drakhalion.** Old god, dragons' patron, watcher over Creator-made dragons of Halig. Native to the world.

### Ro'ak's four lieutenants — kill order

1. **Ser Kael Sunstrike** — Lieutenant I. Crownhaven Lord Commander, returned in scorched royal steel. Killed on the road past Greenwatch (Region 1).
2. **Seraphiel Moonwhisper** — Lieutenant II. Fallen elven Council member of Elenathir. Killed at the Cradle (Region 2). Dies for good.
3. **Vorthak the Crimson Destroyer** — Lieutenant III. Noctheris-made dragon (a purpose-built exception, NOT a Creator-native dragon). Killed on the Demesne Moors (Region 3).
4. **Durgan Cinderforge** — Lieutenant IV. The one dwarf who stayed below. Ancient, warm, insane, hostile. Reveres Noctheris. The hardest of the four. Killed at the Black Spires (Region 4).

## Locked lore conventions — DO NOT VIOLATE

1. **Spelling: Valerian (NOT Valerion).** User explicitly chose Valerian. All files are corrected.
2. **The Mothers wield no magic.** They are ordinary women who give their unborn children to Noctheris. They pay the price; Noctheris does the magic.
3. **Ro'ak is unique.** He is the ONLY Mother-given child to survive infancy and grow. Every other child the Mothers have offered Noctheris was consumed as a sacrifice. There are no other grown Mother-children walking Halig.
4. **Other vessels exist, but they are adult bargainers.** Seraphiel, the scattered Mother-cult witches, the wandering rite-workers who poison wells and bind strawmen — they are grown men and women who sold themselves to Noctheris later in life. They can channel her gift; she can reach through them; they serve in Ro'ak's army. But they were never her children. That word belongs to Ro'ak alone.
5. **Dragons are native Creator-made creatures of Halig.** Drakhalion watches over them. Aetherion (Old Forest, Region 2) is a Creator-native dragon. **Vorthak (Region 3) is the exception** — purpose-built by Noctheris. Dragons are exceedingly rare, always have been; most sleep in places no road reaches.
6. **Vallen is a Skye, not Valerian blood.** House Valerian is extinct.
7. **Crownhaven fell in Year 0 AC.** The current year is Year 500 AC. The game opens in spring of 500 AC; the endgame is winter 500 AC.
8. **No emojis. No italic markdown.** Bold and headers are fine.
9. **No image generation in this project.** User is determining art style independently and does not want lore entries pre-anchored by AI-generated visuals.

2. **The user answers with letters or short text** (e.g., "1a, 2b, 3 — keep it but rewrite, 4 confirmed").
3. **The assistant then drafts the full text inline in the chat — not a summary, not bullet highlights — the actual prose the user would read in the file.** The user has explicitly said: *"from now on show me the actual draft so i can read it."* Do not skip this step.
4. **The user reviews the inline draft and says "lock in"** when they're satisfied. Only then is the change committed.
5. **NEVER infer mechanics, systems, or lore from context.** The user said: *"don't do that again"* — always ask directly.
6. **After "lock in":** write the change to the appropriate `.md` file, present the updated file inline or as a download, and (if a GitHub repo is connected) commit + push and present the commit URL.
7. **If the user catches an error, acknowledge it and fix it before continuing.** Do not defend bad drafts.

### Endgame structure (locked)

- **Region 4 capstone = Ro'ak**, in his High Hall at the summit of the Black Spires.
- **When Ro'ak dies**, the Cinder-Hearth in his hall dies, the floor cracks open, and Noctheris pulls his body down into her prison-chamber.
- **Region 5 begins** with the descent into that chamber — a vertical journey through Hollowing-broken dwarven works and sleeping halls.
- **Region 5 capstone = Noctheris**, in Mother's Hearth, in her own prison.
- The four other Old Gods (Visstheri, Aqueloth, Drakhalion, Morhaem) watch the Noctheris fight from behind the cracks. They do not act. They take notes. Sequel hook.
