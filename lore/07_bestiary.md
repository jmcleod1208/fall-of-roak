# Bestiary — Creatures of Halig (Spring–Winter 500 AC)

> The threats the party will face on the road east. Every creature here is either a natural being twisted by corruption, a willing servant of the Old Gods, or a thing Durgan Cinderforge built. **Nothing in this world exists outside the Creator's making — but plenty has been bent away from it.**
>
> Organized by biome/region. Each entry is structured for both lore and gameplay use — appearance, origin, behavior, abilities, weaknesses, encounter notes.

---

## Region 1 — The Greenfields

*Fernfield, Hartwick, the Crown Road, and the patchwork of farmland, woodlot, and pasture across the western half of Eadenreach. The first ground the party walks. Threats here are mostly human, mostly recent, mostly the leftovers of a kingdom that fell three months ago. The corruption that ruined Crownhaven is only now creeping westward — most of what stalks these fields is what people became when their world ended, not what Noctheris's vessels made them.*

**Threat tier:** Low to moderate. This is where the player learns the game. Drops are common-tier — wool, iron, herbs, leather, salvaged Crownhaven steel, simple relics from burned chapels.

---

### 1. Crown Road Brigands

**Appearance.** Mixed bands of 4-8 in mismatched gear — a Crownhaven cuirass over a farmer's tunic, a hunter's bow paired with a soldier's shortsword, a captain's helm dented and resold three times. Faces are gaunt. Eyes are tired. They are not theatrical villains. They are men who decided three weeks ago that robbing travelers was easier than starving.

**Origin.** Disbanded Valerian militia, refugees turned predator, deserters from the Crownhaven garrison who fled before the city fell. Most of them were honest men in Spring. By Autumn they will have killed a dozen travelers between them.

**Behavior.** Set up at chokepoints — bridges, fords, hedgerow bends. Demand coin or food first. Fight only if pressed, but fight to the death once committed because surrender means hanging at Hartwick. Will scatter and re-form rather than die en masse.

**Abilities.** Mundane. Bow ambush from cover, shield-and-spear front line, a captain with a real sword and real training. No magic.

**Weaknesses.** Morale. Drop their captain and half the band breaks. Faith-fire from Van's gauntlets terrifies them — they have never seen real holy magic.

**Encounter notes.** Earliest fights in the game. Teaches the player loadout, positioning, AI toggles. **Drops:** copper crowns, dented Valerian blades, leather armor, dry rations, the occasional sealed letter that hints at the wider collapse. Recurring farmable encounter — different bands keep forming along the road.

---

### 2. Fallen Reeve's Company

**Appearance.** A larger, more organized brigand band — 10-15 strong, led by a former village reeve in a black-dyed Valerian tabard with the crown insignia cut away. He still carries his oath-staff. He still calls his men "the company." He has not admitted to himself what they have become.

**Origin.** A reeve from a small Greenfields hamlet who lost his village to a refugee swell, then to a raid, then to fire. He gathered survivors, decided the king was dead and the law was his, and now extorts caravans "for the protection of the realm." He is not Noctheris-touched. He is just a man who chose wrong.

**Behavior.** Disciplined. Holds formation. Will parley before fighting and will accept tribute. If refused, fights with real coordination — shield wall, archers behind, the reeve himself in the center. Treats his men's lives as currency.

**Abilities.** Trained Valerian combat doctrine. The reeve has minor command-buff aura (rallies routing men, AI re-engages). No magic.

**Weaknesses.** The reeve himself is the lynchpin. Without him the formation collapses. Vallen — a Fernfield man, not Valerian blood, but a hunter the reeve once knew by name in the old days — can shake the company with a single straight word: he reminds the reeve who he was before the fire, who his men were, what oath he is still pretending to keep. A scripted moment the first time, an AI buff on re-runs.

**Encounter notes.** Mid-Greenfields. A morally hard fight — these are not evil men, just broken ones. **Drops:** Valerian company-steel (a tier above brigand gear), the reeve's oath-staff (caster relic), formal documents (lore unlocks), one of the better early-game shields.

---

### 3. Hollow Farmer

**Appearance.** A man or woman in muddied homespun, walking the wrong way down a furrow at dusk. Skin sun-cured to leather. Eyes a flat grey-violet. They do not speak. They carry a sickle, a pitchfork, a threshing flail — whatever was in their hand when Noctheris's whisper reached them.

**Origin.** **The first creep of corruption westward.** A farmer who lost a child to plague, a wife who lost a husband to the road, an old man who lost everything when Crownhaven burned. Noctheris's whisper found their grief. They did not bargain. They just *listened*, and let her in.

**Behavior.** Wanders. Attacks anything that comes within ten paces. Does not pursue beyond its field. Returns to its plot when the threat is gone. Some are seen tending crops — *the wrong crops, in the wrong rows, watered with something dark.*

**Abilities.** Surprising strength for a peasant. The sickle cuts deeper than it should. Bleeding wounds from a Hollow Farmer's tool **resist Calypsa's healing** for an hour after combat — minor lingering debuff.

**Weaknesses.** Slow. Solitary. Faith magic dissolves the violet film over their eyes — they collapse, weeping, suddenly themselves again. (Most die from the shock. A few survive and are taken to Hartwick for the priests.)

**Encounter notes.** First "supernatural" enemy in the game — the moment the player realizes the threat is bigger than bandits. **Drops:** raw wool, root vegetables (consumable healing), the occasional corrupted seed (alchemy reagent), a strip of black-stained cloth (rebuild material — the chapels need it for purification rites).

---

### 4. Plague Crow

**Appearance.** Crow the size of a hunting hound. Feathers black-on-black with a violet sheen at the edges. Eyes milk-white. The beak is wet.

**Origin.** Ordinary crows that fed on corpses left from the fall of Crownhaven and the burning of border villages. The corruption that ruined the bodies passed through the meat into the birds. They grew. They are still growing.

**Behavior.** Flocks of 3-6. Circle a target. Dive in pairs from opposite sides. Go for eyes and throats. Will scatter when half the flock is killed and reform an hour later.

**Abilities.** Aerial harassment — hard to hit with melee, fast enough to dodge most arrow shots. A bite carries a minor **carrion-rot** debuff (slow HP drain over 10 seconds, stacks up to 3).

**Weaknesses.** Holy fire annihilates them — they go to ash mid-flight. AOE faith abilities are highly efficient. Heavy crossbows can pin one mid-dive.

**Encounter notes.** Common roadside random encounter. Teaches the player about ranged/aerial threat handling. **Drops:** corrupted feathers (alchemy + village ward materials), a meaty body (food, low quality), occasionally a small worked-metal trinket the crow stole from a corpse (low-tier accessory).

---

### 5. Bog-Boar

**Appearance.** A wild boar the size of an ox, hide caked in pale clay, tusks the length of a man's forearm. Eyes red-rimmed and intelligent in a way a pig's eyes should not be.

**Origin.** Twisted wildlife. The bogs west of Crownhaven took the corruption first because the city's runoff drained through them. The boars drank from the wrong puddles.

**Behavior.** Solitary, territorial. Charges in straight lines, but the charge is *terrifyingly fast.* Will retreat to its wallow if wounded badly and re-emerge healed days later (resets between player visits).

**Abilities.** Goring charge (heavy single-target hit, knocks down). Tusk slash (bleed). Thick hide reduces incoming arrow damage by half.

**Weaknesses.** Slow to turn. Vulnerable on the flanks. Faith fire ignites the clay caked on its hide and panics it.

**Encounter notes.** A tank-busting solo encounter. Teaches positioning and AI flanking toggles. **Drops:** thick boar-leather (armor crafting, valuable for villages), tusks (weapon components / decorative trade goods), bog-clay (ward material), generous meat.

---

### 6. Wraith of the Hearth

**Appearance.** A pale, half-translucent figure of a woman in a burned apron, standing at the doorway of a ruined cottage. Her face is featureless except for the suggestion of a mouth. She does not speak. She *waits.*

**Origin.** Most of these are **mothers (lowercase) who died defending their children when Crownhaven and the border villages burned.** Their souls would normally have gone to the Creator. Noctheris **caught them on the way** — pulled them sideways at the moment of death — and pinned them to the threshold of the homes they died protecting. They do not know they are dead. They are still waiting for someone to come home. *(Note: not Mothers-with-a-capital-M. These are ordinary women who loved their children, not women who gave them up.)*

**Behavior.** Stationary until approached. When the party crosses the threshold, she attacks. If driven from the door, she fades and reforms at the same doorway the next night.

**Abilities.** Cold-touch (HP drain, ignores armor). Wail of grief (party-wide minor fear debuff — slight accuracy drop). Cannot be killed by mundane weapons — only faith magic, blessed steel, or fire properly dispels her.

**Weaknesses.** **Banishing rite** — Van can speak a Creator's blessing that releases her cleanly. She thanks him as she goes. This is the *good* outcome. Killing her with raw force works but leaves a residue on the threshold that other corruption will exploit.

**Encounter notes.** Found in burned hamlets along the Crown Road. **Drops:** soul-glass shard (rare crafting component, a piece of her caught essence — used in high-tier ward objects), the small possessions she died holding (locket, wooden toy — rebuild materials for chapels and memorial markers).

---

### 7. Whisper-Touched

**Appearance.** Outwardly normal villagers, merchants, soldiers. The mark is in the eyes — a faint violet ring around the iris, visible only at certain angles. They wear no obvious symbol. They blend.

**Origin.** People in the Greenfields who heard Noctheris's whisper and **listened.** Not yet vessels. Not yet powerful. Just *receptive,* and slowly becoming more so. **The Mothers** — the cult's hidden lay network of women who long ago paid the price — cultivate them as spies, informants, and sleepers, drawing them deeper toward Noctheris over months and years.

**Behavior.** Encountered as quest-givers, travelers, supposed allies. Will betray the party at scripted moments — leading them into ambushes, poisoning food at an inn, opening a gate at the wrong moment. **Combat encounters happen when the player catches them in the act.** Statistically weak in a fight, but the Whisper-Touched often have armed friends nearby.

**Abilities.** No magic of their own (yet). They are dangerous through *information* and *position* — they know where the party is camping, what the party is carrying, who the party trusts.

**Weaknesses.** Faith magic reveals the violet ring instantly. Van can flag them across a marketplace. Once exposed, they break and run.

**Encounter notes.** Recurring scripted appearances that teach the player to be suspicious of friendly NPCs. Re-runs in farm mode replace specific traitors with generic "Whisper-Touched cell" combat encounters. **Drops:** hidden Mother-tokens (lore + ward materials), encrypted letters (lore unlocks), small purses of unmarked silver.

---

### 8. Cottage Spider

**Appearance.** A spider the size of a large dog, body the color of soot, legs banded with rust-red. Webs are not white — they are a thin, oily violet, and they smoke faintly when burned.

**Origin.** Common house spiders that nested in the rafters of homes where the Mothers gathered in secret. **The Mothers themselves wield no power** — but their devotion drew Noctheris's attention, and her residue lingered in those rooms for years. Generations of exposure to that residue twisted the spiders living in the eaves. They are not magical. They are *adapted* to corruption.

**Behavior.** Lairs in attics, cellars, woodsheds, barn lofts. Drops on prey from above. A nest will have 1 large mother-spider and 6-12 small juveniles. Active at dusk.

**Abilities.** Venomous bite (DOT poison, modest). Web-shot (single-target root, breakable). The juveniles swarm; the mother-spider flanks.

**Weaknesses.** Fire (any fire — not just holy). Open ground (they need rafters or cover). Bright light startles them.

**Encounter notes.** Indoor encounters during village rebuild missions and abandoned-cottage clears. **Drops:** Cottage-spider silk (valuable textile, far stronger than wool — a key rebuild material for ropes, bowstrings, light armor), venom glands (alchemy), chitin (light armor components).

---

### 9. Strawman

**Appearance.** A scarecrow that should not be moving. Sackcloth head with stitched mouth and button eyes. Body of bound straw and broomhandle. One in three has a real human jawbone sewn into its mouth.

**Origin.** Field-wards put up to guard hidden Mother-shrines in Greenfields hamlets. The bind itself is whispered by **a wandering vessel of Noctheris** — a grown child of one of the Mothers, carrying her gift through the countryside — who passes through, speaks the rite over the strawman, and moves on. The wards keep guarding their assigned ground long after the vessel has left and long after the shrine has been abandoned.

**Behavior.** Stands utterly still in a field at noon. Walks at dusk. Will not leave the boundary of its assigned field. Will not ignore intruders. Slow but tireless.

**Abilities.** Heavy two-handed swings with a broken scythe or hoe (slow, telegraphed, brutal). Immune to fear, bleed, and most morale effects. **Two HP bars** — the straw body, and the jawbone "core" that has to be destroyed after.

**Weaknesses.** Fire (annihilates the straw stage instantly, then forces the jawbone core into a much weaker fight). Holy magic (severs the Noctheris-bind, drops it where it stands).

**Encounter notes.** Field-clearing missions during the Fernfield and Hartwick rebuilds — every field needs to be cleared before villagers can re-plant. **Drops:** straw (basic rebuild material in bulk), the jawbone (rare ward-purification reagent), occasionally a hidden Mother-token or scrap of map.

---

### 10. Hartwick Stray Pack

**Appearance.** 5-10 dogs of mixed Greenfields breeds, mangy, ribs showing, eyes still mostly themselves but going. The pack leader is larger, scarred, and has begun to show the violet eye-shine.

**Origin.** Refugee dogs and farm dogs whose owners died or abandoned them in the spring chaos. They have been surviving on corpses. The pack leader has been at it longest, and is the furthest gone.

**Behavior.** Coordinated pack hunters. Surround. Cut off retreat. The leader directs from the rear. Will retreat as a unit if reduced below half, then return that night with the same tactics and more rage.

**Abilities.** Mundane — bite, knock-down, encircle. The pack leader has a low-grade fear aura.

**Weaknesses.** Kill the leader and the pack scatters permanently (until the next pack forms elsewhere). The dogs can be **calmed** with cooked meat + a Calypsa nature-affinity check — a non-combat resolution that the game tracks.

**Encounter notes.** Outside Hartwick walls and along the Crown Road. **Drops:** pelts, meat, the leader drops a corrupted-collar (low-tier accessory) and occasionally a chewed-up identifying token from its dead owner (lore + small rebuild moments — "this collar belonged to the miller's daughter").

---

### 11. Sour-Well Sludge

**Appearance.** A waist-high mass of black-violet ooze pooled at the bottom of an abandoned well or cistern. Smells of rot and old iron. Bubbles softly. Has no face, but seems to *consider* a person looking down at it before it moves.

**Origin.** A water source contaminated by **a single Noctheris-rite performed at the well by one of her vessels** during the Spring chaos — a wandering Mother-child who stopped, spoke into the dark, and walked on. The sludge is what has grown there since. The residue of that one rite, slowly thickening over weeks, slowly becoming aware.

**Behavior.** Stationary unless the well is approached. Will climb the well wall to attack. If destroyed, the well is reclaimable; if ignored, it spreads into the village's water supply and the whole hamlet gets sick.

**Abilities.** Acid-touch (armor damage over time — corrodes equipment, can degrade gear quality if the fight goes long). Engulf (single-target restrain + DOT). Splits into two smaller sludges at half HP.

**Weaknesses.** Fire (yes). Faith magic burns it cleanly. Salt thrown into the well before combat halves its HP — a small prep mechanic that rewards exploration of the village before engaging.

**Encounter notes.** Found during well-purification missions in Fernfield and Hartwick rebuilds. **Drops:** purified well-stone (key rebuild material — every village needs at least one), reagent-grade alchemical residue, occasionally a drowned offering at the well's bottom (lore item — a Mother-token, a child's shoe).

---

### 12. Pilgrim-Husk

**Appearance.** A figure in pilgrim robes walking the Crown Road alone, hood up, head down. Carries a walking staff and a small bundle. Looks, from a distance, exactly like a faithful traveling to Hartwick for shelter. Up close: the robe is empty below the chest. The head is real. The hands are real. **The body between is hollowed out.**

**Origin.** Faithful pilgrims caught and killed by **a Mother-cell** (the Mothers themselves do the catching, the hiding, the body-keeping — they wield no power), and then handed to **a wandering Noctheris-vessel** who performs the partial-raising. What is raised is not the person (see `06_systems.md` — Mother-children and other vessels can drag the dead back partly, but never wholly). The husk keeps the pilgrim's face and the pilgrim's path. **It does not know it is dead.** It continues toward Hartwick, where the cult wants it to deliver a message, a curse, or a body.

**Behavior.** Walks the road in one direction only. Will not engage unless spoken to or blocked. When engaged, fights with the staff and surprising strength — the hollowness inside reaches out as black tendrils. Will keep trying to continue toward Hartwick even mid-combat — must be killed, not driven off.

**Abilities.** Tendril strike (single-target pull-in). Pilgrim's-voice (begs for help in the dead man's voice — a fear/hesitation debuff that resists Vallen's command). Resistant to bleed and crit (no organs to wound).

**Weaknesses.** Faith magic — Van can sever the Noctheris-thread and lay the pilgrim properly to rest. Doing so cleanly grants a small **Creator's-blessing** buff to the next combat. The pilgrim's spirit, finally free, thanks the party in a half-whispered prayer.

**Encounter notes.** First **undead variant** the party encounters. Establishes how Noctheris-necromancy looks in practice. **Drops:** pilgrim's staff (low-tier caster weapon), pilgrim's bundle (rebuild material for chapels — sacrament wine, a sealed scripture, alms-coin), and a fragment of the original soul preserved as a soul-glass shard (high-value rebuild reagent).

---

**Greenfields summary.** Twelve creatures. Mostly human-derived (brigands, hollowed farmers, husks, traitors), with the first hints of true supernatural threat (wraiths, husks, sludges) creeping in. Bestiary teaches loadout, positioning, AI toggles, terrain interaction (wells, fields, doorways), and rebuild-material farming. No dragons here — the Greenfields are too sunlit. Dragons are reserved for the Spires or the Eastern Wilds.

## Region 2 — The Old Forest

*The vast woodland east of the Greenfields. Ancient. Layered. The canopy holds its own weather. The party enters at **Greenwatch** on the western fringe, where a friendly elven ranger detachment still keeps faith with the old courtesies. East of Greenwatch the forest darkens, the songbirds thin out, and the moss begins to glow faintly violet at night. At the green heart of the wood, behind walls of woven living trees the party will never see open, stands **Elenathir** — sealed against the rot. Between Greenwatch and Elenathir, the corruption is spreading from east to west, leaking outward from **Seraphiel's Cradle**, the enclave the fallen high elf has carved into a once-sacred glade. Every creature in this region is either holding the line, hiding from her, or already hers.*

**Threat tier:** Moderate to high. The first region with overt corruption-magic on the wild itself. Friendly elven followers can be recruited here. Drops shift toward uncommon-tier — moon-silver, heartwood, faintly luminous resins, fallen-elf relics, and the first proper enchantment reagents.

---

### 1. Greenwatch Rangers (Allied / Recruitable)

**Appearance.** Tall, lean, hooded elves in mottled green-grey cloaks over light scale of woven bark and silver thread. Longbows of single-grown yew. Curved leaf-bladed knives. Faces are calm and tired. They do not raise their voices.

**Origin.** A Council-ordered detachment posted at the western fringe of the Old Forest. They have held Greenwatch through the fall of Crownhaven, the closing of Elenathir's gates, and Seraphiel's eastward poisoning of the wood. They are still loyal to the Council. They consider Seraphiel personally dead to them and will not say her name.

**Behavior.** Allied. The party is received with courtesy if they arrive cleanly — no fallen-elf blood on their hands, no Noctheris taint on their gear. Greenwatch offers safe camp, water, news of the eastern wood, and a handful of standing bounties (the **uncorrupted Aetherion scale** is one of them, see below).

**Abilities.** Master archery (precision shots, multi-shot, vine-rope arrows that root targets). Forest-walk (no terrain penalty in brush). Quiet-cant — they coordinate in near silence, no Vallen-style command shouts needed.

**Recruitable.** Yes. A small number of rangers will follow the party as **followers** after a Greenwatch quest chain is completed. They use ranger AI (kite, harass, root, never melee). They will not enter Elenathir with the party (the party never enters anyway), and they will refuse to engage Seraphiel personally without Council sanction — a flag that pays off in the endgame.

**Encounter notes.** First friendly major NPC group of the region. Establishes Old Forest etiquette, the elven side of the war, and gives the player their first non-human follower options. **Drops (from quest rewards, not combat):** moon-silver arrowheads, woven-bark cloaks (light armor with stealth bonus), elven trail rations, map fragments of the inner wood.

---

### 2. Fallen Elf Warden

**Appearance.** Once a Greenwatch-equivalent ranger from a different posting, now turned. Cloak rotted to a wet black-green. Skin pale to the point of luminescent. Eyes a flat violet, no white left. Bow still strung with what was once silver, now tarnished to soot. Moves wrong — too smooth, no body sway, like something puppeting a body it understands but does not feel.

**Origin.** Elves who answered Seraphiel's call when she defected, or were taken later. Her gift, channeled through Noctheris's vessels, hollows them of grief and fills them with directed hunger. They are not undead. They are alive, awake, and willing — which is worse.

**Behavior.** Hostile on sight. Engages at extreme range, falls back through cover, never stands still. Hunts in pairs or trios with overlapping fire lanes. Will use the forest itself — vine-snares Seraphiel has woven into the wood respond to them.

**Abilities.** Pierce-shot (armor-bypass on first hit). Whisper-arrow (a thin violet keening that applies a stacking despair debuff at range). Vine-call (cues a corrupted root-snare from the terrain, brief root + bleed).

**Weaknesses.** Open ground. Faith-fire (Van) strips the violet sheen from their eyes mid-fight — for a moment they look like themselves again, then collapse properly. Vallen's command voice does **not** work on them; they do not answer to human kings.

**Encounter notes.** Core fallen-elf grunt of the region. Common, dangerous in numbers. **Drops:** corrupted moon-silver (smelt-down reagent), tarnished elven bowstaves, violet-resin vials (caster reagent), and rarely a **memory-leaf** — a pressed leaf an elf carried from home, used in elven follower personal quests.

---

### 3. Fallen Elf Bladesinger

**Appearance.** Lithe duelist in layered bark-scale, twin curved blades of darkened moon-silver, hair bound back, mouth open in a continuous near-silent song that does not match any breathing pattern a living throat should produce.

**Origin.** The Bladesinger order was once Elenathir's high martial discipline — a blade-and-voice combat form taught only to Council-rank warriors. Seraphiel was one. Those who followed her brought the form with them, and Noctheris's corruption has unbound it from the discipline that kept it controlled.

**Behavior.** Closes distance fast. Engages front-line characters in duels. Will isolate one party member and try to force a 1v1, ignoring others until the chosen target is downed.

**Abilities.** Bladesong (a sustained vocal hum that buffs their own speed and parry, debuffs nearby party speech-based abilities including Vallen's command shouts). Twin-strike (paired hit, second blow is enchanted to bypass shields). Step-between (short blink-dash through forest shadow).

**Weaknesses.** Sustained pressure. Their song requires breath; AOE stagger or interrupt cancels the buff cleanly. Faith-fire applied to their blades briefly cuts the corruption-link and reduces their damage for the rest of the fight. Roak's brute strength matches up well against their finesse.

**Encounter notes.** Mini-elite. Appears in the deeper forest in pairs or as a guard for higher-tier fallen-elf encounters. **Drops:** twin curved moon-silver blades (paired dual-wield set, party-best at this point), bladesinger sash (light-armor caster relic), and a **silenced score** — sheet music that, restored at Greenwatch, unlocks a counter-song buff for elven followers.

---

### 4. Fallen Elf Thornsinger

**Appearance.** Robed caster, barefoot, hair grown long enough to drag in the moss. Hands stained dark to the elbows. Sings in a low woven voice that makes the forest answer — vines twitch, roots shift, thorns lengthen.

**Origin.** Elenathir's grove-keepers were druidic gardeners — they tended the living architecture of the city. The fallen ones still know the work. They just garden for Seraphiel now, and the wood she wants is a wood that kills.

**Behavior.** Back-line support. Stays behind fallen-elf melee. Reshapes terrain mid-combat — grows thorn-walls, root-pits, branch-spears.

**Abilities.** Thornwall (raises a hostile bramble line that splits the arena). Root-grasp (single-target root + slow bleed). Wake-the-wood (animates a nearby corrupted plant — see Verdant Husk, below — as a one-time summon per fight).

**Weaknesses.** Fire of any kind disrupts her song. Van's faith-fire is devastating. Cut her line of sight to her terrain and her shaping fails. She is physically fragile.

**Encounter notes.** First proper terrain-shaper enemy. Teaches the player to break sight and rush back-line. **Drops:** thornsinger circlet (caster relic), heartwood reagents (high-tier crafting), and a **grove-seed** — a corrupted seed that Greenwatch can purify into a healing-grove buff item.

---

### 5. Verdant Husk

**Appearance.** A human or elven body so overgrown with moss, root, and vine that the original shape is mostly suggestion. Pale flowers bloom from eye sockets. Walks in lurching steps. Sometimes there is a faint heartbeat-pulse of violet light deep in the chest cavity.

**Origin.** Travelers, hunters, refugees, and the rare unlucky elf who died in the corrupted forest and were not properly tended. Seraphiel's gift, soaking into the soil, claimed them. They are closer to plant than person now. Calling them "undead" is not quite right — they are *re-grown*.

**Behavior.** Slow on open ground, fast through brush. Drawn to noise and warmth. Travels in clusters of three to six. Will burrow into the leaf-litter and ambush from underfoot.

**Abilities.** Burst (on death, sprays violet spores — a small AOE poison-DOT). Root-lash (whip-attack with a vine-arm). Grow-back (if killed without burning, will re-knit from a surviving root inside 24 in-game hours unless the corpse is cleansed).

**Weaknesses.** Fire. Cleansing rites (Van). Salt-circles around camps prevent ambush. The violet heart-pulse is a vital point — a precise strike there kills cleanly.

**Encounter notes.** Region 2's hollowed-foot-soldier analog. Establishes the regrow mechanic that pushes the player toward fire and faith-magic loadouts. **Drops:** violet spore-sacs (alchemy reagent), heart-pulse cores (caster reagent), the occasional intact pendant or coin from whoever this used to be.

---

### 6. Moss-Wraith

**Appearance.** A half-seen shape in the deep moss — a hooded outline, sometimes mistaken for a deer or a sleeping pilgrim until it lifts a face that is mostly hollow. Trailing wet-green tendrils. Carries no weapon.

**Origin.** Old Forest hunters and rangers who died alone in the wood, never recovered, and whose grief was strong enough that Seraphiel's spreading sorrow gave them a thin second-life. Distinct from the human Hollowed Pilgrim in the Greenfields — these are forest-grief, not Mother-grief.

**Behavior.** Stalks the party at twilight. Will not attack a full party in daylight. Targets isolated members at camp or at night.

**Abilities.** Sorrow-touch (lifedrain on contact, applies a mourning debuff that lowers max stamina until cleansed). Moss-step (silent movement, no footstep sound cue). Vanish-into-leaf (escape if hit hard, returns later).

**Weaknesses.** Light. A lit lantern within ten paces breaks its veil. Faith-fire ends it cleanly with a faint, grateful exhale.

**Encounter notes.** Night-encounter and camp-threat. Teaches the player to keep watches set and lanterns lit. **Drops:** moss-wraith veil (light-armor stealth relic), grief-water vials (alchemy), and rarely a ranger's surviving locket or carved name-token that Greenwatch will recognize and reward.

---

### 7. Corrupted Stag of the Inner Wood

**Appearance.** Once an iconic great stag of the Old Forest — pale grey coat, ten-point rack. Now: the coat is patched with violet-black moss, the rack has grown into a jagged crown of thorny antler-bone, and the eyes are gold gone wrong (the gold has bled into the white). Still beautiful, in a way that hurts to look at.

**Origin.** The great stags were Creator-made beasts the elves treated as kin. Seraphiel's corruption of the central forest reached them last. The few that survive are no longer kin to anything.

**Behavior.** Territorial. Charges from cover. Will not retreat. Calls smaller corrupted wildlife (wolves, boars — see below) to its aid mid-fight.

**Abilities.** Antler-charge (high-damage line attack, knocks back). Thorn-spray (cone AOE bleed). Call of the Sick Wood (summons 2-3 corrupted forest beasts as a one-time fight summon).

**Weaknesses.** Light wounds bleed it badly — its corrupted blood resists clotting. Sustained ranged pressure. Faith-fire visibly pains it; the elves consider killing it cleanly an act of mercy.

**Encounter notes.** Mini-boss of the mid-forest. Cinematic encounter. **Drops:** thorned antler (high-tier crafting reagent, central to a Greenwatch quest), corrupted hide (uncommon armor base), a single **clean heart-vein** that Van can sanctify into a one-use cleanse charge.

### 8. Forest Pack (Corrupted Wolves)

**Appearance.** Old Forest grey wolves, now lean to the point of skeletal, coats matted with violet moss-rot, mouths foaming dark. Run in packs of five to eight.

**Origin.** Native wolves caught in the spreading corruption. Driven mad and hungry. They no longer hunt to eat — they hunt because they cannot stop.

**Behavior.** Aggressive coordinated pack tactics — flankers, lead biter, two trailing harassers. Will retreat under heavy fire and return at night.

**Abilities.** Flank-bite (bleed). Pack-howl (pack speed buff, refreshes on each ally kill). Rabid bite (chance to apply mild corruption-disease, stamina-drain until cleansed).

**Weaknesses.** Killing the lead wolf collapses the pack — survivors scatter. Fire breaks pack discipline. Roak's intimidation aura makes them hesitate.

**Encounter notes.** Standard recurring wildlife threat. Numerous and farmable. **Drops:** corrupted wolf pelt (light armor crafting), fang strings (alchemy), and rarely a **clean alpha pelt** (an alpha whose corruption hadn't fully set in — Greenwatch buys these for a bounty).

### 9. Boar of the Black Loam

**Appearance.** A massive corrupted boar — twice the mass of any natural pig — with tusks the length of a forearm and skin that has gone dark and waxy. Where it gores something, the wound smokes faintly violet.

**Origin.** Native Old Forest boar twisted by drinking from a corrupted stream. Solitary now — even the corrupted pack-wolves leave it alone.

**Behavior.** Charges in straight lines. Will smash through small trees. Reckless. Fearless. Will not retreat.

**Abilities.** Crushing charge (high single-target damage, ignores light armor). Tusk-gore (heavy bleed). Black-loam stamp (briefly poisons the ground in a small area — standing in it drains stamina).

**Weaknesses.** Predictable charge lanes — sidestep and counter. Faith-fire applied to its tusks neutralizes the smoke effect. Cannot turn quickly.

**Encounter notes.** Optional mini-boss roaming the mid-forest. Repeatable kill (population, not unique). **Drops:** giant tusk (weapon-craft material, central to one of Durgan's commissions if you bring him a clean one), black-loam-tainted hide (heavy armor base after cleansing).

### 10. Hollow Hawthorn

**Appearance.** A standing dead hawthorn tree, ten to fifteen feet tall, twisted into something almost humanoid — two long thorn-laden limbs, a hollow knot where a face would be, root-tangle for a base. Bone-pale wood streaked with violet sap.

**Origin.** A Thornsinger's first work, or the work of one long since dead — a tree the corruption has *taught*. Once animated, they persist on their own.

**Behavior.** Stationary until approached, then uproots in a slow lurch. Will not pursue beyond its grove.

**Abilities.** Thorn-rain (large area projectile attack). Grasp-roots (single-target root with bleed). Sap-burst (on death, splashes corrupted sap in a small radius — minor damage and a temporary slow).

**Weaknesses.** Fire (devastating). Heavy crushing damage from Roak or Durgan-built siege gear. The face-knot is a vital weak point.

**Encounter notes.** A mini-elite that anchors small corrupted-grove encounters. Often guards a Thornsinger or marks a path toward Seraphiel's Cradle. **Drops:** violet-sap heartwood (rare crafting reagent), thorn bundles (alchemy), and a **grove-token** that Greenwatch can use as a divining tool to map Cradle approaches.

### 11. Cradle Acolyte

**Appearance.** A fallen elf in deeper robes than the rangers — Seraphiel's inner-circle livery. Cowl drawn, hair shorn. Carries a thin staff of bound silver and corrupted oak. Speaks in long sentences in old Elenathin that the party cannot understand but Greenwatch follower-elves can.

**Origin.** Elves who served Seraphiel personally at the Cradle and were sent outward as missionaries-of-a-sort, spreading her corruption deeper into the forest's edge. Channelers of her gift but not its source — they are conduits, not casters of their own power.

**Behavior.** Operates in groups of one acolyte + 3-4 fallen-elf melee. Stays near the back. Will retreat into the deep wood if her escort dies.

**Abilities.** Cradle-channel (heals nearby fallen-elf allies with sickly violet light). Seraphiel's-borrowed-whisper (a far-reaching debuff that targets a single party member with an isolating fear effect — the rest of the party fades to grey in that character's vision for several seconds). Living-vine-armor (passive self-defense — bramble grows on hit, returning a small portion of melee damage).

**Weaknesses.** Faith-fire. Cutting her off from line-of-sight to the wood weakens her channel. Her staff is the focus — destroying it ends her channel mid-fight.

**Encounter notes.** Field commander rank. Tougher than a Thornsinger because the channel ability sustains her side mid-fight. Forces the party to prioritize her. **Drops:** Cradle-staff (high-tier caster relic), inner-circle robes (caster armor), and a sealed **Cradle-letter** — a fragment of Seraphiel's correspondence that fills in her arc.

### 12. The Briarchild

**Appearance.** A small, almost child-shaped figure made of woven thorn, root, and pale leaf. Two glowing violet motes for eyes. Stands no higher than waist height. Laughs sometimes, in a sound that is almost a real child's laugh and not quite.

**Origin.** Seraphiel's grief-experiment. The Council never let her bear children of her own — there is a long, deeply private reason for this in her backstory. After her fall, in the Cradle, she has tried to *make* one out of the wood. The Briarchild is what she gets. She loves it. It is also a hateful thing. Both are true.

**Behavior.** Appears unpredictably in the deep forest, never near the Cradle itself. Hostile but skittish — will dart into brush, taunt, and re-emerge somewhere else. Not directed by Seraphiel; it acts on its own corrupted impulses.

**Abilities.** Tangle (instant root). Mocking-laugh (taunts the party's focus onto itself for several seconds — a debuff that reverses Vallen's command-target). Brier-shroud (becomes briefly untargetable inside a sudden bramble cloud).

**Weaknesses.** Fire. Patience — its taunt and shroud both have cooldowns and it cannot sustain combat for long. Killing it cleanly leaves a small pile of normal wildflower seeds and a hush in the air, as if something just-barely-real has stopped being.

**Encounter notes.** A roaming side-encounter, optional. A morally hard kill — the lore is bleak. Triggers a Calypsa companion moment if she is in the party (she has thoughts about a thing that should not have been). **Drops:** briar-shroud cloth (high-tier stealth-caster relic), wildflower seeds (one of which Greenwatch will plant on a Council-grave, unlocking a small permanent buff), and — if Calypsa was present at the kill — a unique cutscene tag.

### 13. Aetherion the Green, the Raging Wyrm of the Old Forest

**Appearance.** A sinuous, serpentine forest dragon — fifty paces nose-to-tail, no wings, a true wyrm built for canopy and ground. Mossy green scales darkening to a violet-edged sheen along the spine and crest. Long jaw, double-row of recurved teeth. Eyes that were once gold are now gold shot through with violet, and they do not focus — they sweep, hunting. Steam rises from his hide as if the corruption inside him is too hot for his skin. He does not roar. He **screams** — a high, sustained, wounded sound that is the worst noise in the game.

**Origin.** Aetherion is a **Creator-made native dragon of Halig**, older than the Crownhaven dynasty by several centuries. He chose the Old Forest as his sleeping ground in the time before the elves built Elenathir, and he slept in a deep glade at the wood's green heart for nearly a thousand years. The elves of Elenathir always knew of him. They kept his glade quiet. The Council referred to him formally as *the Sleeping Wyrm*.

*Drakhalion's order over the dragon-kind was already broken by Drakhalion's own fall — but ancient dragons like Aetherion mostly simply slept through the long centuries, and Halig forgot most of them. He was supposed to sleep through this age too.*

Seraphiel, after carving her Cradle near his glade, did not assault him directly. She **poured her gift into his glade slowly** — into the soil he rested on, the water that seeped beneath him, the air that pooled in the hollow. Years of it. Decades. By the time the eastern forest began visibly to rot, Aetherion was already compromised in his sleep. He woke not turned, but **broken** — his ancient mind unable to separate his own pain from the world's pain. **Noctheris and Ro'ak, working through Seraphiel, can drive their corrupted subjects to rage, and Aetherion is the largest mind they have ever bent that way.** He is not Seraphiel's servant. He is a wounded ancient being driven mad with corrupted fury, lashing outward at everything.

**Behavior.** Hostile from the first instant the party enters his glade. **No parley. No negotiation. No moment of clarity.** He rages — sustained, unrelenting, screaming throughout — and will not disengage. He attacks everything that moves, including occasionally the wood itself (uprooting trees, blasting the glade open as the fight progresses). The arena visibly degrades as he tears it apart.

**Abilities.**
- **Greenfire breath** — a wide cone of green-violet flame. Burns hot enough to ignite living wood. Heavy AOE.
- **Serpent-coil** — a sweeping body attack that crosses the entire glade in one motion, knocking back and rooting anyone caught.
- **Canopy-snap** — leaps to the upper canopy, drops onto a single target with a thunder of broken branches. High burst.
- **Scream of the Sick Wood** — a sustained vocal attack, applies a stacking pain/disorientation debuff to all party members within line-of-sight. Forces the party to break cover periodically.
- **Rage-spasm** — every time he loses a major portion of his health, he convulses and his attacks accelerate. The fight gets faster the closer he gets to death, not slower. He is not pacing himself. He is dying loud.

**Weaknesses.** **Van's faith-fire is the hard counter.** When her flame strikes him, the violet sheen visibly burns off his scales in patches — for a few seconds those patches are normal dragon-green again, and damage to them ignores his scale armor. Faith-fire is also the only thing in the game that briefly cuts through his scream debuff. **Vallen's command voice does not affect him** — he is far too old and far too far gone for a human king to touch.

**Encounter notes.** Region 2 capstone fight. Cinematic. Long. The arena is the dying glade itself, which Aetherion progressively destroys as the fight wears on. Killing him is grim — Greenwatch's captain, told of it later, simply nods once and looks at the ground for a long time. The elves do not celebrate. **Drops:**
- **Aetherion's heart-scale** — a single scale from above the heart, faith-fire-burned clean of corruption by the killing blow. A legendary crafting reagent (Durgan can forge a near-best weapon from it).
- **Greenfire gland** — high-tier alchemy reagent.
- **An uncorrupted throat-scale** — a small scale from the underside of his throat that Seraphiel's gift never reached. **Greenwatch will take this for proper burial of the Wyrm's name.** Turning it in unlocks a permanent passive ("Quiet of the Sleeping Wood" — small forest-encounter stealth bonus) and a unique line of mourning-song from the Greenwatch captain. This is the only quest in Region 2 the elves consider truly important.
- **Aetherion's tooth** — Roak claims this if he's in the party and wears it. It does nothing mechanically. He just wants it.

---

**Old Forest summary.** Thirteen creatures. Heavy emphasis on fallen elves (a single faction, Ro'ak's army, with distinct classes — warden, bladesinger, thornsinger, acolyte), corrupted wildlife (wolves, boar, stag, hawthorn, husks, moss-wraiths), one tragic invented creature (the Briarchild), one allied recruitable faction (Greenwatch Rangers), and Aetherion — Region 2's capstone, a Creator-made native dragon driven to rage by Noctheris and Ro'ak working through Seraphiel. The region establishes terrain-shaper enemies, night-camp threats, the regrow mechanic, and the player's first non-human follower options. Friendly elves can be recruited; no parley is ever offered by the corrupted side. Seraphiel herself is not yet fought here — she is the Region 2 storyline antagonist, but her boss fight is staged elsewhere in the campaign.
