# Part 1 — The Heroes of Fernfield

## The Skye Family & Vander de la Cruz

Three of the four heroes share the surname **Skye** — one of the oldest families in Fernfield, woven into its soil since before Vallen's grandfather's time. The fourth, Vander de la Cruz, has been a brother to the family in everything but blood since boyhood. They are the four who walked east together the morning after Fernfield burned.

---

### 1. Runlan Skye — *The Hunter, the Father, the Tactician*

- **Age:** 47
- **Role:** Ranged DPS — longbow + hunting dagger. **Tactician of the party.**
- **Faction:** The Greenfields / Skye family
- **Profession:** Hunter and gatherer for Fernfield. Provides game, herbs, and medicinal plants to the village.

**Appearance:**
- **Height:** 6 feet.
- **Build:** Lean and muscular, with **broad shoulders** earned from forty years of drawing a longbow. The body of a man who has worked outdoors his entire life — every muscle is functional, none of it for show. Carries himself like a deer in a meadow: still, watchful, ready.
- **Hair:** Long gray hair, the color of weathered iron, falling past his shoulders. *Geralt's hair from* The Witcher 3. He pulls it back when he hunts. A **medium-length gray beard**, kept tidy but never groomed for vanity.
- **Eyes:** The eyes of **Aragorn from *The Lord of the Rings*** — a steady, patient gray-blue that has seen weather and grief and doesn't flinch from either.
- **Skin:** Fair, **Scandinavian**-toned — pale beneath the tan that years in the sun have laid over it. Wind-roughened across the cheekbones.
- **Bearing:** Stands tall. **Shoulders back, chin level.** A stoic posture that comes naturally, not from training.
- **Face:** Stern by default. He does not smile easily, but when he does — usually for Calypsa, sometimes for Vallen, often for Van — the whole face changes.
- **Voice:** **Low and gravelly.** The voice of a man who speaks rarely, so when he speaks, people listen. Slight rasp from years of cold mornings.
- **Everyday clothing (Fernfield):** Dark wool tunic, leather hunter's jerkin, sturdy trousers, knee-high boots. A patched green cloak when the weather turns. A bow always within arm's reach.
- **Travel gear (the road east):** Leather hunter's jerkin reinforced at the shoulders. A heavier dark green hooded cloak — practical, Greenfields-made. Quiver across his back, longbow over one shoulder, hunting dagger at the hip.

**Heritage:**
- **Father:** **Henry Skye** — a hunter who knew every deer-path and every birdcall in the Old Forest's western fringe. Taught Runlan the bow before he could read.
- **Mother:** **Rose Skye** — a butcher and fur trader. The hands behind the family's livelihood. She and Henry made a clean pair: he brought it home, she made it useful.
- Both passed away peacefully years before the burning. Runlan buried them himself, in the small cemetery behind Fernfield's chapel.

**Faith and values:**
Henry instilled two truths in Runlan that have shaped every choice of his life:
1. **The Creator is real, and He sees.**
2. **Family is the most important thing in this world. Nothing comes before it.**

Runlan does not preach. He is not a man of long speeches. But he prays before every hunt, and he has never once put anything above Calypsa or Vallen. **He never will.** When the village burned, he did not weep. He gathered his family, his bow, and his friend, and he started walking east.

**Personality:**
Quiet. Thoughtful. Patient as only a hunter can be. He listens before he speaks. He plans before he acts. He is the **tactician** of the party — the one who reads the terrain, picks the ambush spot, scouts ahead, knows when to fight and when to slip past.

He is not cold. With Calypsa he is warm and unguarded. With Vallen he is firm but proud. With Van he is loose and laughing, the only person besides his wife who can pull a real grin out of him.

**Combat (auto-battler context):**
- **Primary weapon:** Longbow — accurate, patient, picks off priority targets.
- **Secondary:** Hunting dagger — close-quarters when forced.
- **Role:** **Ranged DPS** + **tactician.** In game terms, his presence shapes party formation and target priority. Possibly grants party-wide buffs related to scouting, terrain, or ambush.
- **Combat philosophy:** Strike first, strike from cover, end the fight before the enemy knows it began. *"A clean shot is a kindness."*

**What he carries:**
A longbow Henry made him at fourteen. A small wooden carving of Calypsa, made the year they married. A folded cloth from Vallen's first hunt.

### 2. Calypsa Skye — *The Heart, the Healer, the Mother*

- **Age:** 48
- **Role:** Healer / Support — sword and shield, herbalism, field medicine. **The party's heart.**
- **Faction:** The Greenfields / Skye family
- **Profession:** Homemaker, nurse, and herbalist for Fernfield. One of the village's "wives and mothers" — the women whose unspoken work keeps a village alive.

**Appearance:**
- **Height:** 5'6".
- **Build:** **Lagertha at her most striking** (Katheryn Winnick at her prime). **Slim, lithe, athletic** — toned but slender, graceful rather than heavy. The build of a woman who has worked her body her whole life: hauling water, tending herb gardens, lifting patients, walking the fields. Lean muscle under fair skin. **Beautiful in a way that men notice and women respect** — the kind of striking that does not need to announce itself.
- **Hair:** **Honey-blonde**, sun-warmed — a shade richer than wheat. **Long and flowing**, kept loose in the home and worn in a **thick warrior's braid** when she's traveling or tending wounds, with loose strands deliberately framing her face. Sometimes a single braid down the back; sometimes a crown braid woven across the top with the rest spilling free.
- **Eyes:** Piercing **pale blue** — calm water that can hold a stare. Eyes that have wept and laughed equally and come out steady. **The first thing strangers notice. The thing they remember.**
- **Skin:** **Fair**, with a faint warm sun-color across the cheekbones from her years in the herb garden. A small constellation of freckles across the bridge of the nose — Vallen has them too, smaller. Smooth, well-kept; she has aged well.
- **Bearing:** **Elegant and composed.** She moves with an unhurried grace — the kind that draws the eye without trying. Even in working clothes, even with flour on her hands, there is a poise to her that the rougher folk of Fernfield notice and gentle their tone around.
- **Face:** **Strikingly beautiful.** High cheekbones, a strong elegant jaw, full lips, a soft confident half-smile that is almost always present. Smile lines at the corners of her eyes from a life spent laughing more than crying. The kind of face that calms a room before she has said a word — and turns heads when she walks into one.
- **Voice:** **Soft and warm.** Carries further than expected when she needs it to — she can call a child home from across a field without raising her tone. Sings while she works.
- **Everyday clothing (Fernfield):** A linen dress in homespun colors — pale green, cream, soft brown. An apron worn over it, often dusted with flour or stained with herbs. A wool shawl in cooler weather.
- **Travel gear (the road east):** **Fitted chain mail** tailored close to her figure over a deep **forest-green tunic** — sturdy for the road, but cut to her shape in the way a shieldmaiden's gear should be. A **wide leather belt** cinched at the waist. A **one-handed sword** at her hip that she would rather never draw, an herb pouch on the opposite side, and a healer's kit at the small of her back. A **round wooden shield** strapped across her shoulders, painted with a small symbol of the Creator. **A simple silver pendant** rests at her collarbone — a gift from Runlan the year they married. Her braid spills forward over one shoulder, loose strands at the temple.

**Heritage:**
- **Father:** **Francis** — a fisherman from the western coast where the Greenfields meet the sea. Brought in his catch every dawn, sold it before noon, mended his nets every dusk.
- **Mother:** **Alexandra** — a fishmonger who worked beside Francis at the market stalls. The voice that called the prices, the hand that wrapped the fish.
- Both passed peacefully years before the burning. Calypsa visited their graves on the coast every summer, until the year she met Runlan.

**Heritage of the sea:**
Calypsa grew up with stories of **Aqueloth, the Hullbreaker.** Her parents never met him — they were small folk, working a small bay, never far from harbor. But the old fishermen told tales, and Francis and Alexandra passed those tales on to their daughter as **cautionary stories about the ocean.** *"Don't sing the wrong song on a still night, Calyp. The Hullbreaker hears."*

She believed the stories were just stories. She is going to learn otherwise.

**Faith and values:**
Calypsa's faith is quieter than Runlan's but no less deep. Where he prays before action, she prays in the small moments — over a sick child's forehead, over a wound she's stitching, over the bread she sets out for dinner. **She believes the Creator is in the small things.**

Her family is everything to her. *Everything.* She would walk into fire for any of the three men beside her, and they all know it.

**Personality:**
Warm. Wise. Sees everyone. The kind of woman who notices a child crying in a crowd and knows, just by looking, what that child needs. She is the social and emotional center of the party — the one who keeps Vallen from getting too cocky, Van from drinking too much, and Runlan from going too quiet.

**She prefers not to fight.** She has a sword and shield because every Greenfields woman of her generation learned to handle one, but she would always rather mend than strike. **When she does strike, she strikes to protect, not to kill.** Her sword is for keeping enemies off her patients.

**Combat (auto-battler context):**
- **Primary weapon:** Short sword + round shield — defensive stance, holds the line near the back rank.
- **Secondary kit:** Herb pouch, bandages, salves — heals the party between fights and during.
- **Role:** **Healer / support.** Restores HP, removes status effects, may grant a small buff tied to "the Creator is in the small things" (a passive party-wide regen, perhaps).
- **Combat philosophy:** Stand near those who need her. Strike only when they reach her. *"Every wound mended is a small victory."*

**What she carries:**
A small carved fish her father whittled for her. Alexandra's recipe book. A pressed sprig of the rosemary that grew outside the Skye home.

### 3. Vallen Skye — *The Son, the Striver, the Quick Blade*

- **Age:** 20
- **Role:** Jack-of-all-trades — specializes in **speed.** Sword, bow, dagger — uses what the moment calls for.
- **Faction:** The Greenfields / Skye family
- **Profession:** Apprentice hunter under Runlan. Helping his father provide for the village.

**Appearance:**
- **Height:** 5'10". Half a head shorter than his father, which Vallen has not yet stopped noticing.
- **Build:** **Slim and muscular** — the build of a young hunter. Quick, lean, agile. Like **Anduin Wrynn from the *War Within* cinematic**: a young king's build — not bulky, not slight, but *honed.* Athletic without being heavy. Built for speed.
- **Hair:** **Short, blonde** — his mother's color, kept much shorter than hers. Practical for hunting and travel. **Short beard** of the same blonde, a few shades darker than the hair on his head. A young man's beard, well-kept but not yet quite full.
- **Eyes:** Inherited his mother's pale blue — but with his father's intensity behind them. People who have known both Runlan and Calypsa say Vallen has *Calypsa's eyes wearing Runlan's expression.*
- **Skin:** Fair like both his parents. Sun-color across the brow and forearms from working outdoors with Runlan.
- **Bearing:** **Carries himself with confidence** — chin up, shoulders square. Some of it is his father's training. Some of it is youth. Some of it is the genuine self-assurance of a man who has been loved well by his family his whole life.
- **Face:** Handsome, open. The face of a young man who has not yet been hurt by the world. *That changes over the course of the journey.* Calypsa's freckles, smaller. Runlan's jawline.
- **Voice:** **Strong and commanding.** Surprises people the first time they hear it — they expect a young man's voice from a young man, but Vallen's voice has weight to it. He speaks clearly, projects without effort. *(He does not yet know how much like a leader he sounds. He will learn.)*
- **Everyday clothing (Fernfield):** A simple linen tunic, leather jerkin, sturdy trousers, work boots. Often muddy. Often with a bow over his shoulder, in conscious imitation of Runlan.
- **Travel gear (the road east):** Light leather armor — not heavy plate, nothing that slows him down. A traveling cloak of forest green. A short sword at the hip, a hunter's dagger at the small of his back, a short bow across his shoulders. He carries less than his father — by design, for speed.

**Heritage:**
Son of Runlan and Calypsa. Only child. Their entire world.

**Faith and values:**
Vallen has a strong faith in the Creator — but where his father's faith is the steady belief of a man who has lived 47 years inside it, Vallen's faith is **the eager faith of youth.** He wants to use it. He wants to see it work in the world. **He looks to Van as the model of what an active, channeled faith looks like.**

Van is teaching him slowly — not magic itself (Vallen does not yet have the gift, or has not yet found it), but the *posture* of faith: the daily prayer, the discipline of belief, the way a believing man carries himself when the world is dark.

**Personality:**
**Ambitious.** **Restless.** A young man looking for two things:
1. **A wife** — he wants to start his own family, settle down, build the kind of home Runlan and Calypsa built. He is not in a hurry, but he is looking. *(May meet someone during the journey. May not. Open thread.)*
2. **A name for himself** — he wants to matter in the world. He wants the songs to know him. He wants Runlan, when Runlan is old, to brag about him at the hearth.

This ambition is held in check by his father's quiet hand. Runlan has taught him patience the way Henry taught Runlan: by waiting longer than the boy thinks reasonable, until the boy understands. Vallen is **restrained, not without fire.** The fire is there. The discipline is what makes him formidable.

**Combat (auto-battler context):**
- **Primary weapons:** Light sword + short bow + dagger — switches mid-fight. **Speed-focused.**
- **Role:** **Jack-of-all-trades, specializing in speed.** Quick attacks, high evasion, opportunist strikes, mobility around the battlefield. Can flank, can chase down fleeing enemies, can close gaps to enemy archers, can fall back to the line when needed.
- **Combat philosophy:** Be where the enemy is not. Strike where the enemy is weakest. *"Father says the deer don't see the arrow that takes them."*

**What he carries:**
A sword Runlan gave him on his eighteenth name-day. A small bow Henry made before he died. A worn copy of a passage of scripture Van wrote out for him by hand.

### 4. Vander "Van" de la Cruz — *The Smith, the Priest, the Friend*

- **Age:** 47
- **Role:** Tank — mace or flail + heavy shield. **Faith-channeling battle priest.** Holy fire, holy shield, healing, smiting.
- **Faction:** The Greenfields / Skye family (by bond, not blood)
- **Profession:** Blacksmith by day. Lay priest by night.

**Appearance:**
- **Height:** 6'6". The tallest man in Fernfield by half a head.
- **Build:** **Broad-shouldered, husky.** A blacksmith's body — heavy across the chest and shoulders, thick-armed, strong-handed. Not lean like Runlan; **dense.** The kind of build that closes the front line of a fight just by standing in it.
- **Hair:** **Long, black, shoulder-length.** Often loose, sometimes pulled back with a leather cord when he's at the forge. Thick, with a slight wave to it. **A large, full black beard to match** — thick and broad across the chest, well-kept but generous. The first few silver hairs are starting to show at the chin.
- **Eyes:** Dark brown, warm. Eyes that crinkle at the edges from forty-seven years of laughter.
- **Skin:** **Light brown** — a Mediterranean / Mexican tone, inherited from his great-grandparents who crossed the sea generations ago. Stands out in the Greenfields, where most folk are fair. He has never been a stranger here — Fernfield knew his father George and his grandfather before him — but his coloring marks him visibly as **a man of foreign blood, settled deep into Greenfields soil.** He carries it without self-consciousness. It is simply who he is.
- **Bearing:** Big and warm. Walks with a slight forward lean, as if always ready to clap a friend on the shoulder. Takes up space without dominating it. Around children, he hunches down to their level without being asked.
- **Face:** **Kind by default.** Broad, square jaw under the beard. A nose that has been broken once (boyhood scuffle with Runlan, neither of them remembers what it was about) and set well. When he smiles, the whole face joins in.
- **Voice:** **Loud and kind.** Carries across a village square. Booms when he laughs — and he laughs often. Soft when he prays, low and steady. Two voices that come from the same chest: the smith's roar and the priest's whisper.
- **Everyday clothing (Fernfield):** A linen shirt rolled to the elbows, leather smith's apron, sturdy work trousers, heavy boots. Soot on the hands, soot on the brow, soot in the beard by the end of a working day. On Sunday: a clean dark tunic, the only fine garment he owns, with a small steel pendant of the Creator's mark at the throat.
- **Travel gear (the road east):** Half-plate over a heavy gambeson — armor he forged himself over the past month, the night-work of a man preparing for what was coming before he had the words for it. A large round shield with the Creator's mark hammered into the steel. A heavy mace or flail at his hip. A pack of forge tools (he can repair the party's gear on the road) and his prayer book.

**Heritage:**
The de la Cruz family is not native to Eadenreach. Van's **great-grandparents emigrated across the sea** from a far southern land — a place of heat, sun, and old cathedrals. They came looking for **green pasture** to start a farm. They settled in the Greenfields three generations ago. The family kept their name, their dark hair, their warmth, and their faith. Van has never seen the country his ancestors left, but the candle on his altar burns the way his great-grandmother taught his grandmother to light it.

- **Father:** **George de la Cruz** — village priest of Fernfield. The chapel was his life. He preached without arrogance, baptized every child in Fernfield for forty years, and sang the hymns louder than anyone.
- **Mother:** **Sophie de la Cruz** — the chapel's other half. She ran every festival, every funeral, every Sunday meal. The village remembered George's sermons. They remembered Sophie's bread.

**The night Fernfield burned:**
George and Sophie were inside the chapel when Ro'ak's host swept through. The chapel was the village's sanctuary; people fled to it expecting safety. The host burned it with the people inside. **George and Sophie died together, holding the door against the flames, trying to buy the people behind them more time.**

Van was not in the chapel. He was at the forge, and then in the streets, fighting. By the time he reached the chapel it was a column of fire.

**Faith and values:**
Here is what makes Van extraordinary: **he keeps his faith.** He does not curse the Creator. He does not blame God for the fire. He grieves — fully and openly — but he does not let the grief turn to bitterness. *"My father died doing the work the Creator gave him. I will do mine."*

He is one of the **few villagers in all of Eadenreach** who can actually **channel his faith into magic.** This is rare — magic in Halig requires belief in the Creator plus aptitude, and aptitude is uncommon. Van has both. His father George had a touch of it. His grandfather had more.

**Van's faith magic — three branches:**
1. **Holy fire** — offensive. A burning light that scorches the corrupted. *"What Noctheris made should not stand in the Creator's light."*
2. **Holy shield** — defensive. A glowing barrier around himself or an ally. Holds against blows that would break a man.
3. **Wards and smitings** — utility. Banishing minor corruptions, sealing doorways against undead, striking a single enemy with sacred force.

Healing is **Calypsa's gift**, not Van's. He can ease pain in a pinch, but the body is her domain. His magic is **light and protection.** Together, Van and Calypsa cover the full healer/support spread — she mends, he shields.

**Personality:**
**Warm. Loud. Loving.** The opposite of Runlan in temperament, which is exactly why they have been inseparable since boyhood. Van laughs easily and often. He brings mead to dinner — *every* dinner. He pours generously. He starts the singing. He tells the embarrassing stories about young Runlan that Runlan refuses to confirm.

He and Runlan met as boys running through the woods east of Fernfield. They got lost together, found their way home together, were beaten by their fathers together for being out past dark. They have not spent a year apart since.

He is **like a second father to Vallen** — the lighter one, the one who lets him have the second cup of mead, the one who tells him stories of his real father from before Vallen was born. *"Your dad once shot a partridge through the eye at sixty paces and threw up afterward because he felt bad for the bird. Don't ever let him tell you he's a hard man."*

Van has been **looking for a wife his whole adult life** and somehow never quite found her. The forge keeps him busy. The chapel keeps him busy. Runlan and Calypsa's house keeps him busy. He is not unhappy. He is open.

**Combat (auto-battler context):**
- **Primary weapons:** Heavy mace or flail + a large round shield, often with the Creator's mark scratched or hammered into the steel.
- **Role:** **Tank.** Holds the front line. Soaks damage. Casts holy shield on himself or allies. Casts holy fire as a counter-attack. Smites priority targets.
- **Combat philosophy:** Stand in front of the people you love. Hold. *"My father held the door. I can hold this."*

**What he carries:**
A small steel pendant of the Creator's mark, hammered out by his father George at his apprenticeship. A worn prayer book in his great-grandmother's old language (he can read it slowly). A flask of mead, always full somehow.

### Family Dynamics

**Runlan and Calypsa** — a marriage of 26 years. They speak in a kind of shorthand. They argue rarely and quietly. They do not need to say *I love you* aloud, though they do.

**Runlan and Vallen** — father and son. Runlan is teaching Vallen the bow, the trail, the silence. Vallen wants to be his father. Vallen wants to be more than his father. Both things are true. Runlan knows.

**Calypsa and Vallen** — mother and son. The bond every mother knows. She still calls him *Val* when no one else is listening. He still lets her.

**Runlan and Van** — brothers in everything but blood. Forty years of friendship. They finish each other's sentences. They go silent together when one of them needs it. Van is the only person Runlan has ever cried in front of, twice — once when Henry died, once the night Fernfield burned.

**Calypsa and Van** — close friends. Calypsa is the only one who can get Van to stop talking when he needs to. Van is the only one who can get Calypsa to laugh when she's grieving. They have a sibling-like trust.

**Van and Vallen** — uncle and nephew, in everything but blood. Van has been at Vallen's table since the day Vallen was born. He held him at his blessing. He taught him to hammer a horseshoe. He is teaching him faith now. Vallen looks to Van the way young men look to the uncle who is allowed to be a little wilder than their father.

**The four of them at a table** — what Fernfield was. What is being rebuilt, slowly, even as they march east.

# Part 2 — The Antagonist

## Ro'ak — *The Warlord, the Vessel, the Wanting Mother's Son*

### Identity

- **Full name:** Ro'ak (born to the Eastern clans; no surname in orc tradition)
- **Age:** 250 years old (born 250 AC, currently 250 in Year 500 AC)
- **Race:** Half-orc — orc father, human mother
- **Role:** **Primary antagonist.** Vessel of Noctheris. Warlord of the host advancing on Eadenreach.
- **Faction:** Ro'ak's Host (commands it directly under Noctheris's guidance)
- **Location:** Fortress beneath the cliffs of Dreadvik, above the unsealed crack the dwarves left in 1000 BC. Now marching west.

---

### Appearance

- **Height:** Over 8 feet tall. He fills doorways. He blocks out the sky.
- **Build:** **As muscular as a minotaur, brutal as an ogre.** A body shaped by 250 years of Noctheris's making — far beyond what half-orc parentage alone would have produced. Slabs of muscle layered over a frame meant for war. Hands the size of a man's chest.
- **Skin:** **Brownish-green** — the orc cast under his mother's human warmth, darkened further by the Hidden Centuries beneath Dreadvik.
- **Tusks:** **Two large tusks** rise from either side of his lower jaw, longer than a pure orc's would be — Noctheris's growth, not his blood's.
- **Eyes:** **Bloodshot.** The whites are not white. They are the color of an eye that has wept blood and never stopped. They pierce the soul. There is something in them that is not entirely Ro'ak — and not entirely Noctheris — but the fused thing the two of them have become.
- **Hair:** Long, black, matted with the oil of war. Pulled back when he fights. Loose when he sits beside her in the dark beneath the fortress.
- **Voice:** **Terrifying. Loud. Raging. Commanding.** *The voice of a true warlord filled with chaotic magic.* It carries above battlefields without effort. It silences orc clans ten times older than him with a single word. When he speaks softly — which is rare, and reserved for Noctheris alone — the voice loses its rage and becomes something tender, almost childlike, and that is somehow worse.
- **Armor:** **Spiked heavy plate** of black iron, forged in the deep places beneath Dreadvik. Skulls and bones decorate the pauldrons, the breastplate, the helm — trophies of two and a half centuries of slaughter. **A long dark robe drapes over the armor**, like a king's cloak, marking him as more than a soldier.
- **Weapons:**
  - **A two-handed axe** in one hand — orc-forged in the old style, oversized for any normal warrior, perfectly balanced for him.
  - **A staff in the other** — taller than a man, made of something that is not quite wood, **glowing purple and green**, dripping with the magical power of Noctheris. The staff does not weigh anything in his hand. It is hers.
- **Presence:** **He does not enter rooms. Rooms become his when he is near them.** Animals run from him. Children scream before they see him. The air around him tastes of old earth and wet rot. He is what is wrong with the world wearing the face of a man.

---

### Origin — The Story of Elena, Ra'gor, and the Bargain

**Ro'ak's story begins not with him, but with his parents.**

#### 1. The Raid (c. 249 AC)

**Ra'gor** was the chieftain of an orc clan in the Eastern Wilds — **powerful, respected, traditional.** Brutal in war, honorable in peace, and, in his personal life, **not cruel.** He held to the old codes. He kept his word.

But Ra'gor wanted a son, and his clan had no female orcs available for mating. **Orc tradition demanded the female choose her mate** — and no one had chosen him. So he did what desperate orc warlords had done for centuries: he led a **raid** on a human village near Crownhaven, looking for supplies, treasure, and a woman who could bear him an heir.

He found **Elena** — a young human woman from a quiet village, ordinary in every way except that she happened to be in the wrong house when Ra'gor's warriors came through.

He took her back to the orc camp.

#### 2. Captivity (249–250 AC)

Elena became Ra'gor's **house slave.** She was kept under his roof, fed, clothed, and — by the standards anyone in the camp would have recognized — **treated relatively well.** Ra'gor was not cruel to her. He never beat her. He spoke to her, occasionally with something close to kindness.

**She was miserable.** She wanted to go home. She **hated him** with the clean, total hatred of a stolen woman. She **looked for escape every day.** Ra'gor kept her locked down, watched her constantly, would not let her near the camp's edge.

The years began to blur. Slowly — and to her own horror — she began to **accept her new life.** She came to **respect Ra'gor's authority and leadership**, even as she hated everything he represented. She did not love him. She would never love him. But she stopped looking at the door every hour.

She became pregnant.

#### 3. The Prayer (during pregnancy, late 249 AC) — *Elena's True Face*

Elena did not want this child. She did not want any of this. While pregnant, alone in Ra'gor's hut at night, she remembered something from her village — **whispers among a few of the older women about the Wanting Mother. About Noctheris.** *"She listens. When no one else listens, she does."*

Elena began to **pray to Noctheris every night.** She prayed for **freedom** — freedom from Ra'gor, from the camp, from the orc life that had swallowed her whole.

**One night, Noctheris answered.** Soft as a mother's whisper. The bargain was simple: **freedom in exchange for the unborn child.**

Elena did not hesitate. **She knew what she was doing.** She had heard the village whispers about the Wanting Mother. She understood the cost. **She was knowingly giving up her unborn son — knowingly damning him — to get what she wanted.**

She did not love this child. She had never wanted him. He was the seed of her captor, growing inside her against her will, and she was a woman willing to use anything — *anyone* — as a tool to escape. **The unborn Ro'ak was not her son. He was leverage.**

This is the harder truth of Elena: **she is her own kind of monster.** Not a tragic woman who didn't know better. Not a victim of misunderstanding. **A woman who, when offered the choice, chose to sacrifice her own unborn child for her own freedom — and did not look back.**

She expected the bargain to take the form Noctheris's bargains usually take: a quiet miscarriage in the night, the pregnancy gone, herself free to walk away. That was the price she had agreed to pay, and she had agreed to it without flinching.

#### 4. The Surprise — *Noctheris Breaks Her Pattern*

To Elena's **shock and dismay, her pregnancy came to full term.** **The child was healthy.** **Ro'ak was born — alive, strong, and entirely as he should be.**

What Elena did not know: Noctheris had **broken her own pattern.** Normally she takes unborn children and absorbs their life force. With Ro'ak, she **saw potential** — a half-orc child born of a violent union, raised by both human and orc, in a world she was preparing to break. **She decided to keep him for something larger.** She would not act yet. She would wait. She would watch what kind of child he became, and choose her moment.

**She held him from the womb. She did not yet whisper to him.**

When Ro'ak was born alive and strong, **Elena was horrified — not because she had failed her son** (she had not even pretended to want him), **but because the bargain she had thought was clean was not clean.** Noctheris had *kept* the collateral. The contract was not closed. Her son was alive, in her arms, and she did not know what was coming for him — only that she had sold him, and the buyer had not yet collected.

She never warmed to him. She could not. **Every time she met his eyes, she remembered the dark hut where she had whispered *yes* to a goddess for her own benefit.** She did not feel guilt — not exactly. She felt **dread.** Dread of the day Noctheris would come for what she had bought.

Ra'gor noticed her coldness toward the boy and never understood it. He thought she was simply broken by captivity. He could not imagine the truth: **the woman in his bed had sold their son before either of them had ever held him.**

#### 5. Ro'ak's Childhood (250–258 AC)

**Ra'gor accepted Ro'ak fully and raised him as his son.** Half-orc or not, Ro'ak was Ra'gor's blood, and Ra'gor was a man who kept his word. He defended his son's place in the clan. He taught Ro'ak the **orc traditions, the clan laws, the codes of strength and survival.** He taught him how to swing an axe before Ro'ak could lift one.

**Ra'gor genuinely loved Ro'ak.** This is the heart of the tragedy. The man who took Ro'ak's mother by force was, to Ro'ak, **the kindest figure of his early life.**

Elena was distant. Cold. Unhappy in a way Ro'ak could feel but not name. She did not love him. She could not look at him without seeing what she had bargained him into.

**Ra'gor was Ro'ak's protector and rock. The one stable thing in a small life.**

#### 6. Ra'gor's Death (258 AC) — The Honor Duel

When Ro'ak was **eight years old**, Ra'gor overheard another orc warrior call his son **weak — because Ro'ak was not fully orc.**

This could not stand. Orc tradition demanded a response. **Ra'gor challenged the warrior to a battle to the death** — the way of the clans. The clan gathered. The warriors squared off in the camp's open ground.

**Ro'ak stood at the edge of the circle. Eight years old. Watching his father fight for his honor and his son's name.**

He was **terrified.** Ra'gor was the only thing he had.

**Noctheris saw her moment.**

She had been waiting eight years, watching, choosing. And here — in this single instant of fear and love and a child standing at the edge of his whole world's collapse — **she struck.** She honored her pact with Elena the only way that served her: **she used Ro'ak as a proxy.** Through the child, she **blinded Ra'gor** in the middle of the duel.

Ra'gor never saw the warrior's club coming. **It caved his head in. He died instantly.**

**Ro'ak collapsed to the ground in agony** — screaming, choking on grief, watching his father's body fall.

#### 7. The Awakening — Noctheris Takes Her Son (258 AC)

The moment Ra'gor died, **Noctheris was empowered and thrilled.** The grief, the rage, the trauma — these are the soils she grows in. **She had been waiting for this.**

The whispers began.

In Ro'ak's mind, in the long terrible silence after his father's body hit the dirt, **a voice** spoke. Soft, vast, motherly. It told him **what he could become.** It showed him visions — armies under his command, kingdoms in flame, a love that would never leave him the way his mother had left him cold and the way his father had been torn away.

He was eight years old. He had no father. He had a mother who would not look at him. He was alone in a clan that had just watched his only protector die for him.

**He submitted.**

**He vowed to destroy everything.**

#### 8. The Journey — Eight Years Old, Across the Eastern Wilds

Noctheris was patient now. She had her child at last. **She knew an eight-year-old could not survive the wilds alone**, so she **guided him.**

She taught him how to **manipulate people** — what to say to a passing trader, what to do to escape a clan scout's notice, when to weep and when to stand still.

She **moved him through the shadows of night**, slipping past dangers that would have killed a grown warrior. She kept him fed, kept him hidden, kept him moving east toward Dreadvik.

**It took weeks. Then months. Then a year.** A small half-orc child, walking through the eastern wilds, guided by the voice of a goddess, headed for the deep places under the cliffs.

He made it.

#### 9. The Lair (259–499 AC) — The Hidden Centuries

**Noctheris revealed herself to him in her lair beneath Dreadvik.**

She told him what had happened. She told him **his mother had given him up.** She told him **Elena had been unhappy that he had survived childbirth.** **Both of these things were true.**

What Noctheris did not tell him — and never has, and never will — was that **she was the one who blinded Ra'gor in the duel.** That his father's death was her doing. That the love of his early life was the price she paid Elena for her freedom.

Ro'ak does not know this. **Even today, at 250, he does not know.** And **at this point, it would not matter.** His loyalty to Noctheris is total.

**For nearly 250 years**, Ro'ak stayed in her lair. **She raised him.** She taught him how he would dominate the world. She fed him knowledge, power, the corruption that strengthens her vessels. She told him he was chosen. She told him he was loved.

**She came to believe — for the first time in her endless existence — that she finally had a child of her own.** A son she had not lost. A child she had not failed to bear. After unknown ages of mothering nothing, **Ro'ak filled the wound.**

#### 10. Elena's Fate

Elena remained in the orc camp after Ro'ak vanished.

When Ra'gor died and Ro'ak disappeared into the wilds, **Elena felt relief.** She showed it openly. The orc clan saw it, and that is part of why they accused her of witchcraft. They were wrong about the *how*, but right about the wrongness in her.

The other orcs noticed her lack of sadness. The whispers began. Eventually the whispers had a name: **witch.** They accused her of using sorcery to kill Ra'gor in the duel.

**One night, a female orc slipped into Elena's hut and cut her head off.**

**Elena had never said a single mother's prayer for her son.** She had not lit a candle for him. She had not asked the gods what had become of him. He was the price she had paid, and she had paid him, and her ledger was clean by her own reckoning.

She never learned where her son went. She never learned what he became. **Her body lies in an unmarked shallow grave** somewhere in the eastern wilds. No one tends it. No one visits. No prayer is said. **No one knows she was the mother of the warlord burning the world.**

**She is one of the great hidden monsters of Halig. No one knows. No one ever will.**

#### 11. Emergence (499 AC — One Year Before the Game Begins)

In the spring of 499 AC, **Ro'ak emerged from the lair beneath Dreadvik for the first time in 250 years.**

He was no longer a child. He was the towering, robe-draped, axe-and-staff-bearing warlord the world will know.

He began **gathering the host.** Some orc clans flocked to him. Some he conquered. Turned humans flowed in. Beasts were corrupted. Wyverns hatched. The dead rose. **Noctheris's making began to walk the eastern lands.**

By the end of 499, his army was vast.

By the spring of 500 AC, **he came west.** Crownhaven fell. Then Fernfield burned.

**The game begins.**

---

### Ro'ak and Noctheris — The Love That Made Him

> *The corrupt core of the antagonist. The player will not be told this directly, but it bleeds through every line he speaks of her, every soft word in the dark.*

Ro'ak loves Noctheris **as a mother and as a wife.** The two loves do not exist as separate things in him — they have grown into each other across 250 years until they are one twisted devotion, inseparable, **and he does not see anything wrong with it.**

**She is the mother he never had.** Elena was cold and distant. Ra'gor died when Ro'ak was eight. The voice in the lair beneath Dreadvik raised him from boyhood through manhood through the unnatural centuries that followed — fed him, taught him, soothed him in the dark, told him he was loved when no one else ever had. **For a quarter of a millennium, Noctheris was the only mothering presence he knew.**

**She is also the woman he wants.** Noctheris reveals herself to him in physical form within the lair — beautiful in a way that is not entirely mortal but close enough to ache. **He is drawn to her physically, and she allows it, and she encourages it.** This is not separate from his motivation to conquer; it is **fused** with it. He fights for her hand at his side. He fights for her voice in his ear. He fights for the warmth of her in the dark under the fortress when the day's slaughter is done.

The wrongness of this — the intimacy with a being who raised him from a child, the tangle of son's-love and lover's-hunger — **never registers to him as wrong.** Noctheris taught him otherwise. She taught him this is what love is. He believes her. He has had no other model of love for two and a half centuries. **He does not know it could have been anything else.**

This is, in part, why he can never be saved. The corruption is not just political or theological — **it is the deepest love of his life, and it is rotten at the root, and he does not know rot from honey.**

---

### Ro'ak's Inner Life

- **Public motivation:** Conquer Eadenreach. Burn the kingdoms of the Creator. Free the Old Gods. Remake the world.
- **Stated belief:** *"The Creator abandoned the world to its own decay. The Old Gods will tend it as it deserves to be tended. My mother will sit at its center."*
- **True motivation:** **Love of Noctheris.** Everything he does, he does for her. The conquest, the killing, the spread of corruption — these are gifts to the only being who has ever loved him without flinching. **He would burn down the stars for her.**
- **Knowledge of the truth:** He believes Elena gave him up. He believes Ra'gor died because the duel went badly. **He does not know Noctheris caused Ra'gor's death.** Even if told, **his loyalty would not break.** Two and a half centuries have made the love unbreakable.
- **Can he be saved?** **No.** This is not a redemption story. There is no boy left inside him to reach. The boy died on the dirt the day Ra'gor's head was caved in. What walks now is what Noctheris built in his place — and what he chose to become.
- **What he fears:** Losing her. Disappointing her. Returning to a silence with no whisper in it.

---

### Personality

- **Charismatic in a terrible way.** He is not a brute. 250 years of Noctheris's tutelage have made him a **persuader, a manipulator, a king of dark arts.** He can speak softly when he needs to. He can convince a doubting captain. He can make a turned human believe their corruption was their idea.
- **Brutal when crossed.** The rage is always near the surface. It is genuine. He felt it first at eight years old at his father's death and he has felt it every day since.
- **Tender — only with Noctheris.** No one else has ever seen this side of him. The few mortals who have caught a glimpse of him whispering to her in the dark beneath the fortress are dead.
- **Patient on a scale mortals cannot fathom.** He waited 250 years to walk back into the sunlight. He can wait another year for the last kingdom to fall.
- **A believer.** He genuinely thinks the world will be better when the Creator's faith is broken and the Old Gods walk free. He is a religious warlord. **This makes him the most dangerous kind.**

---

### Voice / Speech Style (for AI dialogue)

- **Default register:** Loud, commanding, slightly archaic. *"You will kneel, or you will be made to kneel. I have no preference."*
- **When persuading:** Softer, warmer, almost gentle. He has had centuries to practice. *"You have been wronged. I have seen it. I see you. Come — there is a place at her table for you."*
- **When enraged:** Raw, guttural, the voice of a thing not entirely human. The chaotic magic of Noctheris bleeds into the sound. **The air shakes.**
- **When speaking of Noctheris:** Reverent, almost reverent-childlike. He calls her **"Mother,"** sometimes **"my Lady,"** rarely by name. *"My Mother does not lie. My Mother does not abandon. My Mother loves what no one else would love."*
- **What he does NOT say:** He never expresses doubt about Noctheris. He never speaks of Elena except to confirm she gave him up. **He never mentions Ra'gor by name.** Ra'gor is the wound he has buried under 250 years of love for the one who killed him.

---

### Key Relationships

- **Noctheris** — mother, lover, goddess, everything. The center of his existence. He is hers, and she is his, in a love that is the rotten root of the world.
- **Elena** — the woman who gave him up. He believes this is her only sin. He has never sought her out. He believes she is dead — and she is, but he does not know how, or when, or that the orcs killed her in part because of what he became. *He does not know she is the greater monster of his origin.*
- **Ra'gor** — the buried wound. He never speaks of him. He does not consciously think of him. **In a fight, when he raises the two-handed axe, his grip is exactly the grip Ra'gor taught him at five years old.** He does not notice.
- **His lieutenants** — useful tools. Some he respects. Some he tolerates. None he loves. They serve him as he serves her, and that is enough.
- **The orc clans** — he is half their blood, but he is not theirs. The clans that joined him did so because Noctheris's promises were stronger than their elders' counsel. The clans that refused, he intends to crush in time.
- **The party** — he does not yet know they exist. By the time he learns, it will be too late to remove them quietly. They will reach his fortress.

---

### Player Interactions

- **Distant antagonist for most of the game.** The party hears of him constantly — from refugees, from elven scouts, from corrupted lieutenants — but does not see him until late.
- **One possible early glimpse:** Mid-game, perhaps in the Demesne, the party may witness him from a distance — a figure on a ridge, the host parting around him, the staff glowing in the mist. They do not meet. He does not see them. **But the player does, and remembers.**
- **The final confrontation:** At the top of the fortress, above the cracked prison of Noctheris herself. He is the last gate. **And he is not the final boss** — Noctheris is. Ro'ak is the door. Killing him is necessary, but killing him does not save the world. The Wanting Mother still waits beneath.

---

### Why This Matters — The Reversal at the Heart of Ro'ak

The reversal at the heart of Ro'ak's origin is sharp:

- **Ra'gor** — the orc warlord who took Elena by force — was the one who **genuinely loved** Ro'ak. Raised him. Defended him. Died for his honor.
- **Elena** — the human woman, the captive, the one who should have been the wronged party — was the one who **sold her son for her own freedom** without flinching.

This makes Ro'ak's hatred of his mother the most rational feeling he has, and it makes his love for Ra'gor the deepest unhealed wound — buried, unspoken, never named. **The orc was the father. The human was the monster.** This is one of the many quiet ways the game tells the player: *the lines of this war are not racial. They are moral. They are what each person chose.*

And Elena's monstrousness throws Noctheris's role into sharper relief too. **Noctheris was not the worst thing that happened to that child. His mother was.** Noctheris just collected what his mother had freely given.

---

## Noctheris — The Wanting Mother *(canonical manifestation)*

> *"Come closer, child. You have always been mine."*

Noctheris is one of the **Five Old Gods**, sealed beneath the world since before recorded history. She has not walked Halig in her own body in millennia. She speaks through Ro'ak. She works through the Mothers. She is **never seen whole** — until the very end.

But she has a **canonical form**, and the player will glimpse it in fragments throughout the game: in cult statuary, in stained glass at fallen Crownhaven, in Ro'ak's visions, in the dreams of the Mothers, in the cracked-prison flashbacks. The fragmentation is the horror.

### Appearance

- **Tall, shrouded, matronly silhouette.** Always seen through veil, mist, water, or stained glass — never directly. Long flowing veils of black and deep violet drape her form and obscure most of her body.
- **Swollen, gravid pregnant belly** beneath the veils. She is the **eternal hungering mother** — endlessly birthing, endlessly wanting, never sated. The belly is **always there**, no matter what other features shift.
- **A face that is never the same twice.** Sometimes a beautiful young mother. Sometimes an ancient hollow-eyed crone. Sometimes a face Ro'ak almost recognizes — his own mother Elena, his deepest cruelty. The Mothers see the perfect mother they wish they were. Each viewer sees something different. **The face is a mirror, not a feature.**
- **Two violet-green eyes** — burning steady through every veil and every shifting face. **The eyes are the constant.** No matter what she is wearing, the eyes are hers. Once the player learns the eyes, they will recognize her in every shrine, every banner, every statue, every dream.
- **Many slender pale arms** emerging from her shroud at impossible angles — small hands, women's hands, dozens of them. These are **the hands of every child ever given to her** — the children of the Mothers, reaching out from inside her. They reach *outward*, toward the viewer. *Come to me.*
- **A faint purple-green miasma** curls around her bare feet — the same miasma that surrounds Ro'ak, that pours from his staff. It is her presence in the world.
- **A single broken iron chain** trails from one wrist into shadow. The chain that once held her. The chain the dwarves cracked. The chain she has never fully escaped.
- **Backdrop:** when she is glimpsed in vision, she is always in the same place — a vast chamber of cracked black stone, walls split with violet eldritch fissures. **Her ancient prison.** She has not left it. She has only reached out.

### How the player encounters her

- **Cult iconography** — statues in Mother shrines, banners in Ro'ak's host, prayer beads worn by cultists. Always the silhouette and the eyes. Never the full face.
- **Stained glass at Crownhaven** — heretical windows hidden in side-chapels of the fallen capital. The kingdom that fell knew her, secretly.
- **Ro'ak's visions** — when he speaks to her or reaches for her, the player sees fragments through his eyes. He sees her as **mother and wife at once.** Beautiful. His.
- **Dreams of the Mothers** — if the party meets a Mother who tells her story, the player may see Noctheris through that woman's eyes. She is **the perfect mother the Mother wished she could be.** That is how Noctheris caught her.
- **The finale** — at the end of the game, when Ro'ak's vessel breaks, **she manifests fully for the first time.** The player sees her whole. The eyes, the belly, the many hands, the shifting face — all of it, finally complete. **This is the moment of maximum dread.** She has been here all along. She has just finally arrived.

### Voice

- **A woman's voice** — warm, low, motherly. *Soothing.* This is the most disturbing thing about her. She does not roar. She does not threaten. She **invites.**
- Speaks in **terms of endearment** — *child, my love, little one, come.* She calls everyone her child. She means it.
- **Never argues.** Never explains. Never justifies. She *waits.* Centuries are nothing to her. She has the patience of stone.
- **When she is angry**, her voice does not rise. It goes **quieter.** And the air goes cold.

### Why she is so dangerous

Noctheris does not conquer. She does not invade. She **is given.** Every Mother gave her child freely. Every cultist offers worship freely. Ra'gor freely loved Elena, who freely gave Ro'ak. **The horror of Noctheris is that she never takes — she only accepts what is offered.**

This is what makes her impossible to bargain with at the end. She does not want anything from the party. She has already been given everything she ever needed. The party are not negotiating with her. They are **interrupting** her.

### Design note

Noctheris's fragmentation across the game — eyes here, silhouette there, a hand reaching from a stained-glass window — is the long con. The player will see her dozens of times before they realize they have been seeing her. Then the finale arrives, and **she is whole.** The same eyes. The same belly. The same hands. **All in one place. All looking at you.**

# Part 3 — Ro'ak's Lieutenants

The four lieutenants the party will face on the road from Fernfield to Dreadvik. Each commands a region, a division of the host, and a different *flavor* of corruption. Each is a boss fight. Each is a piece of the journey east.

---

## Lieutenant I — Ser Kael Sunstrike *(forest's edge / early Old Forest)*

> *"You think the Creator hears you here? He does not. I prayed too. He left us in the dark."*

- **Type:** Fallen Valerian knight. Former Royal Guard.
- **Region:** **Forest's edge / early Old Forest.** The first true boss of the journey. The party will fight him not long after they leave Fernfield's ruin.
- **Faction:** Ro'ak's Host (commands the turned-human division and the host's vanguard cavalry).

**Origin:**
Ser Kael Sunstrike was once **a sworn knight of the Royal Guard** — one of the elite warriors charged with the personal protection of House Valerian in Crownhaven. He bore the crown's colors. He took the oath at sixteen and held it for thirty years. **He was a good man.**

He was captured by Ro'ak's host **years ago** — early in the host's emergence, when Crownhaven was still standing and most of Eadenreach didn't yet know what was coming. He was taken alive on a border patrol, brought back to Dreadvik, and **subjected to corruption.**

What was done to him in the dark beneath Dreadvik is not entirely clear. Some of it was physical. Most of it was not. **Noctheris and Ro'ak broke him slowly**, the way you break a horse — not all at once, but one piece at a time, until the man who walked back out into the light was wearing Ser Kael's face but was not, in any meaningful sense, Ser Kael anymore.

He was given a choice he could no longer refuse: **fight for Ro'ak, or die.** He fights.

**Personal connection to the party:** None directly — but **his name is known** in the Greenfields. Older Greenfielders remember Ser Kael Sunstrike from before he was lost — the king's young hope, a paragon of Valerian honor. **Runlan, who is 47, remembers his name from boyhood.** Calypsa, who lived briefly closer to Crownhaven in her youth, remembers seeing him at a tournament once. The party recognizes him by his colors before they recognize him by his face.

**The horror:** He **knows** what he was. He has not been mind-wiped. **The original Kael is still in there**, watching out through corrupted eyes, and **he hates what he has become.** But he fears Ro'ak more than he fears damnation, and so he fights, and so he kills, and so he is the deepest tragedy of the journey's first leg.

The party may have **a brief moment of recognition with him** mid-fight or just before death — a flash of the man underneath the corruption, a whispered apology, a please. **He cannot be saved.** Even if they tried, the corruption is too deep, and Noctheris would burn him from the inside before he could turn back. The mercy is the killing blow.

**Appearance:**
- Tall, broad — a knight's build, gone gaunt from imprisonment and corruption. Once strong, now hard-edged in a wrong way.
- **Plate armor that was once gleaming Valerian silver** — the crown of Eadenreach embossed on the breastplate. **The silver has tarnished to black-gray**, and the crown is half-melted, scarred over with darker runes that no Valerian smith would have hammered.
- **Ashen skin**, the color of someone who has not seen the sun in years. Veins beneath the skin run dark — corruption visible in the body itself.
- **Eyes that were once a clear blue, now ringed with ash and shot through with violet light.**
- **A great bastard sword** at his hip — the blade he once swore on. The hilt still bears the Valerian crown. The blade itself **leaks corrupted holy light** — gold corrupted into a bruised, sickly color.
- A long surcoat over the armor, once white with Valerian colors, now gray and bloodstained, never washed.

**Combat flavor — *the dark mirror of Van*:**
Sunstrike fights with **a perversion of the same faith Van channels.** Where Van casts **holy fire**, Sunstrike casts **corrupted holy light** — sickly violet flames that burn the soul as well as the body. Where Van raises a **holy shield**, Sunstrike raises a **shield of dark sanctity** — a barrier that no clean blow can break. Where Van **smites** the corrupted, Sunstrike **smites the faithful.**

This is intentional. **Noctheris built him as an answer to Van.** She has done this with all her best vessels: she finds out what the enemy's strength is, then she makes a vessel that channels the inverse. Sunstrike is a Crownhaven knight whose blade once cut down Noctheris's lesser servants — now he uses the same training and the same faith-channeling discipline against the people he once protected.

**The fight is, mechanically and thematically, Van's mirror.** The player is meant to feel: *this is what Van could have become, if Van had been the one captured*.

**Voice / dialogue style:**
- **Speaks like a knight still** — formal, structured, polite even in violence. The training never left.
- *"Yield, and I will make it quick. It is the kindest thing I can offer."*
- *"You wear Greenfields green. I knew a man from there once."*
- When he glimpses the original Kael through the cracks: a sudden break in tone, almost human. *"I am sorry. I am — I am still sorry."* And then the eyes ring violet again.
- He does not boast. He does not threaten. **He grieves, even as he kills.**

---

## Lieutenant II — Seraphiel Moonwhisper *(deep Old Forest)*

> *"He will rule the new world. I will rule beside him. The forest understands what the Council never could — that the future does not negotiate."*

- **Type:** Fallen elven noblewoman. Former Council member of Elenathir. Witch.
- **Region:** **Deep Old Forest.** The party fights her past the elven scout patrols, in woods she has personally corrupted.
- **Faction:** Ro'ak's Host (commands a division of fallen elves and the corrupted-forest creatures she has summoned and bound).

**Origin:**
Seraphiel Moonwhisper was a member of the **Council of Elenathir** — high-born, ancient by mortal standards (elves live long), a respected voice in the council chamber for centuries. She was originally one of the **"hold tight at Elenathir"** voices: do not send forces out, preserve the city, weather the storm. **She voted to seal the gates.**

But unlike the others who held that position, **Seraphiel did not believe Elenathir would actually survive.** She voted to seal the gates not because she thought the city could outlast Ro'ak, but because she had **already calculated that the human realms would fall first**, and she did not wish to spend Elenathir's last strength on a kingdom already lost.

This was politically defensible. Many councilors thought similarly. **What set Seraphiel apart was what she did next.**

In the long quiet months as the corruption crept closer to Elenathir, she began to **study Ro'ak.** Not as a threat — as a prospect. She watched the host advance. She watched cities fall. She watched the Council despair. **And somewhere in that watching, she made a different calculation: if the new world is inevitable, why not be its queen?**

She fled the capital. She made her way east through the corrupted woods to Dreadvik. She offered herself to Ro'ak — her power, her knowledge, her noble blood, her beauty — as a wife, a queen, a partner in the new order.

**Ro'ak does not love her.** Ro'ak has Noctheris. **He took her offer because she is useful** — her elven magic is centuries deep, and her knowledge of the Council's strategies is invaluable. **He lets her believe she is his queen.** She is not. She is a tool. She does not yet know this. She will not realize it until late, possibly never.

This is one of the great quiet ironies of the host: **Seraphiel believes she is a peer to Ro'ak. She is, at most, a favored knife.**

**Status with the Council:** **Openly disowned.** The Council named her a traitor publicly the day she fled. Her seat is empty and shrouded in black. Some councilors quietly wonder if her treason is what tipped the Old Forest into open corruption — whether she opened doors before she left. *(She did. They are right.)*

**Personal connection to party:** None directly. The party will hear of her from the elven scouts they meet — **with venom.** *"Moonwhisper. Speak that name in our woods and we will kill you ourselves."*

**Appearance:**
- Tall, elegant, terrifyingly beautiful in the way only ancient elves are beautiful. **Ageless face, glacial poise.**
- **Long, silvery-pale hair** like moonlight on water, woven through with twisted black roots and bone ornaments she has added since her fall.
- **Eyes that were once silver, now sheened with deep violet** — the same corrupted hue as Sunstrike's, the mark of Noctheris's chosen. Calmer than Sunstrike's. Untroubled.
- **Robes of dark forest green, blackened at the edges**, embroidered with patterns that seem to shift when watched.
- A **staff of twisted yew**, the wood blackened, the head crowned with the small bleached skull of something that used to be a fawn.
- She wears a thin **silver circlet** — a self-styled queen's crown, a parody of nobility. Ro'ak has never confirmed it. He has never denied it either.

**Combat flavor — corrupted nature magic, summoned forest horrors:**
- **Twists the Old Forest itself** to fight for her — animated tree-roots, thorned vines, woods that close around the party.
- **Summons corrupted forest creatures** — black-eyed wolves, twisted stags with too many antlers, things that were once owls.
- **Throws elven battle-magic** — bolts of corrupted starlight, illusion spells that hide her movements.
- **Difficult to pin down.** Mobile, ranged, control-mage style. The fight feels like the forest itself is the enemy.

**Voice / dialogue style:**
- **Cool, polished, faintly amused.** Centuries of court training did not leave her when she fell. She speaks the way a queen speaks — softly, knowing she will be obeyed.
- Slightly archaic phrasing — old elvish patterns. *"You walk far for a people who were lost the day Crownhaven fell."*
- **Genuinely believes she is Ro'ak's queen.** Speaks of him with possessive warmth. *"My lord will be pleased when I bring him your heads."*
- **Looks down on humans.** Not with hatred — with the mild distaste of a noblewoman watching peasants stumble in her garden.
- When threatened: cold fury, not panic. *"You would raise your hand to me?"*
- **Never says Noctheris's name.** That is Ro'ak's mother. Seraphiel does not like to think about it.

## Lieutenant III — Vorthak the Crimson Destroyer *(Demesne moors)*

> *"BURN. ALL OF IT. BURN."*

- **Type:** True dragon. Fire and shadow. **Creation of Noctheris.**
- **Region:** **Demesne moors.** The dragon hunts the party across open ground, in the mist that has returned to the moors.
- **Faction:** Ro'ak's Host (no division — **Vorthak commands no one**; he is a weapon, not a captain).

**Origin:**
Dragons are real on Halig. They are **Creator-made**, native to the world from the Making, watched over in the old order by **Drakhalion the First Flame.** But they are exceedingly rare — most sleep in places no road reaches, and have for a thousand years or more. **Noctheris cannot reliably find them, and the few she has reached she has had to corrupt slowly, in their sleep.** That work is patient and uncertain. She wanted a dragon she could *command.*

So she made one. **Vorthak the Crimson Destroyer is Noctheris's purpose-built exception** — forged in the deep places beneath Dreadvik over two centuries of dark labor, shaped from the bones and shadow-stuff she has been gathering since before Ro'ak emerged. He is **not** a native dragon. He is **her answer** to native dragons: built to her specification, loyal from the moment he drew breath, hers in a way no Creator-made creature could ever be.

He is what Noctheris attempted when the wyverns were not enough. The wyverns are her infantry of the air; **Vorthak is her terror weapon.** Larger than any wyvern by a factor of three. Older than the host itself — she was forging him while Ro'ak was still in the lair learning her name. **He is the closest thing she has ever made to a true god-creature**, and the closest thing the host has to a tame dragon. The native ones, if any rouse, will not be tame at all.

He is not a beast. **He is a true dragon — intelligent, sapient, speaking.** He understands tactics, irony, fear. He can carry on a conversation. He simply does not particularly want to.

**He serves Ro'ak willingly.** This is important. Vorthak was made by Noctheris and given to Ro'ak as **a son's gift from a mother** — a literal weapon of war handed down in the dark. Vorthak knows this. **Vorthak is grateful.** He owes Ro'ak nothing personally, but he owes Noctheris his existence, and he serves where she points him. **He loves them both — in the limited way a dragon can love.**

**Why fire AND shadow?** Noctheris built him in two halves. **Fire** is the ancient terror of dragons — she gave him the old gift. **Shadow** is her own gift — the corruption she pours into all her makings. The combination is the point: **a dragon that burns and unmakes at the same time.** Where his fire passes, the ground is slag. Where his shadow passes, the corruption seeps into what is left.

**Personal connection to party:** None. He has no idea who the party is. He is hunting them because Ro'ak told him to. The party may be the first creatures to ever speak to him directly without being already corrupted — and he may, briefly, find them **interesting**, the way a hawk finds a clever mouse interesting.

**Appearance:**
- Massive. **Sixty feet from snout to tail-tip.** Larger than any old myth told.
- **Crimson scales** — deep, blood-red, layered like old armor. Shadow seems to cling to the spaces between scales as if smoke were always rising from him.
- **Wings that cast night across whatever they pass over** — even in daylight, Vorthak's shadow is darker than it should be.
- **Two long horns curling back from his skull**, blackened at the tips.
- **Eyes the color of forge-coals** — orange-red with a black slit, ringed by the same violet sheen all of Noctheris's chosen carry.
- **When he breathes, he breathes both fire and a darker thing — a black flame that does not warm, but consumes.**
- His voice rumbles from somewhere deeper than his chest. The ground vibrates when he speaks.

**Combat flavor — fire and shadow, open-ground terror:**
- **Aerial threat** — circles above the moors, swoops to strafe, retreats to height.
- **Fire breath** — sweeping cones of flame that ignite the moor itself, leaving burning ground the party must navigate.
- **Shadow breath** — a darker beam that corrupts what it touches; allies hit by it suffer ongoing damage and may attack each other.
- **Tail and claws** when grounded.
- **Spoken curses** — he can intone short corrupted prayers in mid-fight that buff himself or debuff the party.
- **Open-ground terror** — unlike Sunstrike (close), Moonwhisper (controlled forest), Vorthak fights in the open mist of the Demesne. **Wide arena, no cover, total exposure.**

**Voice / dialogue style:**
- **Deep, rumbling, slow.** Speaks at the pace of a creature who has nowhere to be. Words drag.
- **Surprisingly articulate.** Noctheris taught him language directly. He uses it well.
- *"Small things. Walking far. I have seen this before. They never reach the gate."*
- **Mocking, but not theatrical.** Mockery of the patient kind — the cat with the mouse, not the bully with the smaller boy.
- **Reverent toward Noctheris and Ro'ak.** Speaks of them only with care. *"My maker. My maker's son."*
- When he is finally killed: a long, stretched-out exhale. Not pain. Disappointment. **He thought it would take more than this.**

## Lieutenant IV — Durgan Cinderforge *(Dreadvik gate)*

> *"Aye, I made the gate. I made the towers. I made the cracks in the world wider, lass. The wonder is in the work."*

- **Type:** Ancient dwarf. **Survivor of the Hollowing.** Engineer, architect, fortress-keeper.
- **Region:** **Dreadvik gate.** The last lieutenant. The door before Ro'ak. The party fights him at the entrance to the fortress itself.
- **Faction:** Ro'ak's Host (commands the corrupted dwarven-craft division — golems, automatons, siege engines, the fortress's defensive systems).

**Origin:**
**Durgan Cinderforge is a dwarf who never went north.**

When the Hollowing happened in 1000 BC — when the dwarven mining expedition cracked Noctheris's prison and the surviving dwarves fled in panic and silence to the Far North — **Durgan stayed.** He was a young engineer at the time, brilliant, ambitious, **fascinated by the cracked prison they had unearthed.** Where the others felt only horror and ran, Durgan felt **wonder.** The geometry of the prison was unlike anything he had ever studied. The runes carved into its walls were languages no living scholar knew. **He could not look away.**

He stayed behind to study it. Alone, in the dark, with the crack still seeping the Wanting Mother's whispers.

He has been there ever since. **Roughly 1,500 years.** Noctheris extended his life as she extended Ro'ak's — though for different reasons. Where Ro'ak is her son, **Durgan is her craftsman.** He maintains her lair. He keeps the mountain from collapsing in on her. He builds whatever she asks for. He has been doing it so long that he no longer remembers a world before the dark beneath Dreadvik.

**He is not a slave** — not in his own mind. He could leave, he tells himself. He simply does not want to. **The truth is that 1,500 years of Noctheris's whispered closeness has reshaped him into something that loves her** the way Ro'ak loves her, only quieter, more domestic, the way an old craftsman loves the workshop he has never left. He does not call it worship. He calls it work. **It is worship.** The crack in the world is the most interesting thing he has ever seen, and the goddess inside it is the most beautiful, and he intends to tend her until the work is done or he is dead. He believes both of those things in the same breath, and does not notice they should not fit together.

**He is insane**, in the quiet way of a man who has been alone with a god for too long. The warmth is real — dwarven hospitality runs deep, and Durgan was a hospitable young engineer before he stayed behind. **But it is wallpaper over a wound.** Mid-conversation he will sometimes go still, eyes fixed on something the party cannot see, listening. He talks to her aloud when no one is there. He talks to her aloud when someone *is* there and does not always notice the difference. The friendliness will flip — sometimes mid-sentence — into raw, sudden hostility: a hammer-raised, teeth-bared *get out of her house* kind of hostility, the snarl of a guard dog at the door of a temple. Then he will blink, smile, apologize warmly, and offer the party a sip of something that should not be sipped. **He does not know he is doing this anymore. The Lady is in the room with him constantly, and he has forgotten what a room without her sounds like.**

**This means the dwarves are not entirely silent.** One of them stayed. The party will not learn this from anyone but Durgan himself, and only mid-fight or just before death.

**What he builds:**
- **Siege engines** — ballistae, catapults, bolt-throwers that have been used to break human fortifications across Eadenreach.
- **Defensive turrets** for the fortress itself — automated bolt-throwers, stone-launchers, fire-arrays.
- **The fortress's structural integrity** — he keeps the mountain from crushing Noctheris's lair beneath it. **Without him, the fortress collapses.** This may be tactically relevant — kill him and the fortress begins to fall — though Noctheris has prepared for this contingency.
- **Whatever Noctheris asks for.** New armors. Strange devices. Mechanisms that should not work. He has had centuries to perfect his craft, and Noctheris's whispers have given him techniques no living dwarf would recognize.

**He does not build the bestiary.** That is Noctheris's domain — the witches, the undead, the wyverns. Durgan builds what is *made*, not what is *born* of corruption.

**He knows about the cracked prison. He tends it.** He understands the geometry of the prison better than Noctheris does — she cannot study herself from outside her own bindings. **He is the only being in Halig who fully understands what is beneath the fortress.** This makes him uniquely dangerous, and uniquely valuable to her.

**Appearance:**
- **Short and broad** — even by dwarven standards. Compact, immovable.
- **Skin pale and leathery** — 1,500 years without sunlight. Almost translucent in places, blue veins visible.
- **Beard long, intricately braided, threaded with metal rings.** **The beard is white, but at the tips it has gone *charcoal black* from soot and forge-heat** — a dwarven engineer's beard, marked by his trade.
- **Long hair tied back in a smith's knot.**
- **Eyes a deep, deep amber — like firelight through smoke** — that *look* warm at first glance, the way a forge looks warm. **Look closer and the amber is shot through with the same violet sheen the other lieutenants carry — only deeper, slow-moving, banked beneath the surface like coals.** Her mark is in him as much as in any of them. Maybe more. He has stood in her shadow longer than any other living thing.
- **Forge-burned skin** on his hands and forearms — old scars overlaid with newer ones. He has not stopped working in 1,500 years.
- **Heavy leather apron over plate armor**, both forged by his own hand.
- **Tools at his belt** — hammers, calipers, strange instruments no Greenfields smith would recognize.
- **A great hammer** in one hand, the head twice the size of a man's skull. **A small alchemical pack** at his back — bombs, smokes, acids.

**Combat flavor — the most tactically distinct fight:**
- **Constructs** — Durgan does not fight alone. **He summons or activates iron golems** in waves throughout the fight. The party must manage the constructs while pressuring him.
- **Traps** — the arena around the gate is **his.** He has rigged it. Floor plates trigger spike walls. Bolts fire from concealed turrets. Smoke vents fill the air with corrupted gas.
- **Alchemical bombs** — fire-bombs, acid-flasks, smokes that obscure vision and reveal hidden party weaknesses.
- **Hammer in melee** — slow, devastating, breaks shields and bones.
- **Terrain manipulation** — mid-fight, he can collapse a section of floor, raise a wall, redirect the party. **The arena changes during the fight.**
- **Tactical, not athletic.** Durgan does not run. He does not dodge. He stands in his fortress and **bends the fortress against you.**

**Voice / dialogue style:**
- **Gravelly, old, with a thick dwarven brogue** — vowels rounded, consonants worn smooth by centuries.
- **Warm, almost friendly.** This is the most disturbing thing about him. He chats. He explains his work. He compliments the party's craft (*"Aye, that's a fine bow. Yew? The grain is right."*). **He is not raging. He is enjoying his evening.**
- **Treats the fight as a puzzle.** *"Now this — this is interesting. Four of you, mixed disciplines. I haven't had a four-mix in close to a century."*
- **Fascinated by the party** the way he is fascinated by the cracked prison. He genuinely wants to know how they will solve his arena. He is rooting for the puzzle as much as for himself.
- **Says the word "Noctheris" with deep reverence**, every time — softens the voice, slows the cadence, sometimes touches his chest when he says it. *"The Lady. The Mother. The reason the world is worth standing in."* He will not let the party speak her name carelessly in his hearing. The first time one of them does, his hammer will twitch.
- **The warmth and the rage live in the same sentence.** *"Aye, that's a fine bow — the grain is right, the draw is honest — and you brought it into HER HOUSE without ASKING, lad, what do you suppose she should DO with you for that — "* (deep breath) *"— ah, never mind, never mind. Sit. Have a look at the floor-runes. I'm proud of those."*
- **He talks to her aloud, often.** Mid-fight he will pause to murmur to the air: *"Yes, Lady. Yes. I see them. They are clever. You are right to want a look at them."* He is not bluffing the party. She is genuinely speaking to him. He has not been alone in 1,500 years.
- **He thinks of himself as her husband, in a quiet way he will never say aloud.** Not the way Ro'ak does — Durgan has no illusions of consummation, no fantasy of her body. He is her *husband of the workshop*, the man who has been beside her every day for 1,500 years while she could not move. That, to him, is a closer love than Ro'ak's. He believes this. He is wrong. Ro'ak is her son and lover. Durgan is a tool she has kept warm by hand.
- When he speaks of the dwarves who fled north: **a flicker of something complicated.** Not regret. Not affection. Closer to **bemusement** laced with quiet contempt. *"They ran. Foolish. They never saw what I saw. There was nothing to run from. There was something to *learn.* There was *Her.*"*
- **At his death:** a slow exhale that is half a laugh and half a sob. *"Aye. Well done, lass. Well done, lads."* Then his eyes go past them, to nothing, to her. The warmth drops out of his face all at once. *"My Lady. My Lady — I am sorry. The work — the work is unfinished. I tried. I tried."* He is not speaking to the party anymore. He is not aware they are there. **He dies looking at someone they cannot see, the way a man dies looking at his wife.**

## Why These Four Together

The four lieutenants form a **deliberate progression** of corruption flavors — and each is the dark mirror of something the party values:

| Lieutenant | Corruption Flavor | Dark Mirror Of |
|---|---|---|
| **Sunstrike** | Captivity → Despair | Van's faith |
| **Moonwhisper** | Ambition → Vanity | The Council's wisdom (and any hope of elven aid) |
| **Vorthak** | Loyalty → Made for the dark | The party's bonds (he was made by Noctheris like a child) |
| **Cinderforge** | Curiosity → Fascination | The dwarves who saved themselves (he stayed; they ran) |

**Each fight teaches the player something different about Noctheris:**
1. She **breaks** the faithful (Sunstrike).
2. She **seduces** the prideful (Moonwhisper).
3. She **makes** her own (Vorthak).
4. She **rewards** the curious (Cinderforge).

The four together are the four ways the world is being lost. Past them, only the door, only the warlord, only the goddess remain.

## Aetherion the Green — *The Raging Wyrm of the Old Forest*

> *He was not turned. He was wounded in his sleep, and woke screaming.*

- **Type:** True dragon. **Creator-made, native to Halig.** Ancient. The Old Forest's sleeping wyrm.
- **Role:** Region 2 capstone boss — a mid-game cinematic fight in the deep forest. **Not** a lieutenant of Ro'ak. Not aligned with anyone. A wounded ancient mind driven to rage.
- **Region:** Deep Old Forest, in a glade near Seraphiel's Cradle.

**Origin:**
Aetherion is one of the few **native dragons of Halig** still in existence — Creator-made, watched over in the old order by **Drakhalion the First Flame** before Drakhalion's fall. He is older than the Crownhaven dynasty by several centuries. He chose the Old Forest's green heart as his sleeping ground before the elves built Elenathir, and he slept there for nearly a thousand years. **The elves always knew.** The Council kept his glade quiet, referring to him formally as *the Sleeping Wyrm.* He was supposed to sleep through this age too.

Seraphiel, after carving her Cradle near his glade, did not attack him directly — she knew better. **She poured her gift into his glade slowly**, into the soil he rested on, the water that seeped beneath him, the air pooled in the hollow. Years of it. Decades. By the time the eastern forest began visibly to rot, Aetherion was already compromised in his sleep. **He woke not turned, but broken** — his ancient mind unable to separate his own pain from the world's pain.

**Noctheris and Ro'ak can drive their corrupted subjects to rage**, and through Seraphiel's long contamination they reached far enough into the wyrm's sleep to do it to him. He is the largest mind they have ever bent that way. **He is not Seraphiel's servant. He is not anyone's servant.** He is a wounded ancient being lashing outward at everything that moves, screaming throughout.

**Why he matters thematically:**
Aetherion is the proof that **dragons are still real on Halig**, and the proof that **the old order has been broken from below.** A Creator-made creature of the world, sleeping peacefully for a thousand years, has been hurt in his sleep by something he never consented to fight. The party does not save him. The mercy is the killing blow — and the elves of Greenwatch will accept his uncorrupted throat-scale for proper burial of his name.
